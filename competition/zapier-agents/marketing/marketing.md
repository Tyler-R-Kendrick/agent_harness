# Zapier Agents Marketing

## Audience

- Small businesses, startups, RevOps, support, marketing, and sales teams.
- Existing Zapier users who want AI delegation on top of their app stack.
- Non-technical operators who want agents without building code or infrastructure.

## Positioning

- Core frame: build AI teammates that can do real work across apps.
- Zapier positions agents around delegation, research, task management, and operational assistance.
- The browser is mostly displaced by app actions and knowledge sources.

## Customer Model

- Existing Zapier account and subscription funnel.
- Agent usage is tied to activities, messages, sharing, app connections, and plan limits.
- Business and enterprise expansion follows Zapier's normal team and app-integration buying motion.

## Who They Capture

- Teams already paying for Zapier automations.
- Users whose work happens in SaaS apps with supported actions.
- Buyers who value ease and integration breadth over developer-level automation control.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who need deterministic browser traces, screenshots, and CDP/Playwright escape hatches.
- Users automating websites without reliable app integrations.
- Enterprise teams needing strict action allowlists across all agent surfaces.
