# Orbit Cloud Marketing

## Audience

- B2B teams building web agents and automations that need to move from local development to production cloud operation.
- Enterprises needing auth helpers, audit trails, RBAC, quotas, rate limits, and private deployment.
- Developers who want to keep Playwright, Selenium, or CDP control while gaining managed runtime services.

## Positioning

- Core claim: run agentic automations at scale.
- Market frame: teams need local browser development plus cloud reliability, control, autoscaling, scheduling, and observability.
- Product promise: prototype locally, deploy globally, monitor runs, and enforce operational guardrails.

## Customer Model

- Tailored enterprise pricing based on volumes, regions, and requirements.
- Shared cloud, dedicated clusters, and private/VPC deployment models.
- Likely higher-touch onboarding compared with self-serve browser APIs.

## Who They Capture

- Teams with production automation needs and enterprise controls.
- Buyers who want browser runs as durable operations, not one-off agent demos.
- Organizations already comfortable with cloud deployment and run monitoring.

## Who They Miss

- Hobbyists and small teams looking for transparent pricing.
- End users who want a daily AI browser.
- Developers who want a minimal REST API rather than a full runtime platform.
