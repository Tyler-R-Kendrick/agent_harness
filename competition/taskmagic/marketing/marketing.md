# TaskMagic Marketing

## Audience

- Small businesses, operators, agencies, AppSumo-style lifetime-deal buyers, and no-code builders doing repetitive browser work.
- Users who need to automate clicking, typing, copying, pasting, scraping, downloading, or moving browser data into simple app workflows.
- Teams that find Zapier/Make insufficient because the work happens inside websites rather than only through APIs.

## Positioning

- Core claim: a new era of browser automation with no APIs and no code.
- Market frame: traditional automation tools cannot handle manual website work, so TaskMagic records and replays what a human does.
- Product promise: record once, edit steps, schedule or trigger the automation, and integrate browser runs with app workflows.

## Customer Model

- Free and low-cost paid tiers emphasize unlimited workflows, runs, steps, tags, delays, and users.
- Browser plus Apps bundles create upgrade paths for users who need both page automation and integration workflows.
- Cloud runtime hours, team permissions, and browser recording are part of the value story.

## Who They Capture

- Non-technical browser automation users who want to watch a task get recorded.
- Buyers who prefer "unlimited" messaging over per-action credit systems.
- Users with workflows that start in a browser but need Google Sheets, webhooks, filters, delays, or app-like workflow steps.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who want source-controlled scripts, typed SDKs, CDP-level hooks, or structured replay evidence.
- Enterprises that require strong separation of local credentials, cloud execution, audit trails, and change approvals.
- Users expecting AI to make brittle websites reliable without selector/wait/debug work.
