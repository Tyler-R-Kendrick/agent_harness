# Google Antigravity Marketing

## Target Customer

- Developers and teams adopting an agent-first IDE workflow rather than a traditional autocomplete assistant.
- Frontend and full-stack builders who want agents to write code, run terminals, use browsers, and produce verification artifacts.
- Gemini and Google AI subscribers who want coding-agent benefits inside Google's model and account ecosystem.
- Enterprises evaluating Gemini Enterprise and Google-native agent development tooling.

## Positioning

- "Agent-first development platform" and central command center for AI agents.
- Google positions Antigravity as the next evolution of the IDE: agents are extracted into their own surface and given editor, terminal, and browser tools.
- Product copy emphasizes verification and higher-level communication through tasks and artifacts.

## Customer Model

- Distributed through Google AI account plans with free/paid tiers, weekly limits, and model access differences.
- Enterprise path runs through Gemini Enterprise and Google's broader productivity/developer ecosystem.
- Model optionality is part of the pitch, but Google still owns the shell, account, and orchestration layer.

## Who They Capture

- Users who want a polished agent manager with browser evidence built into frontend workflows.
- Developers who already trust Google accounts, Gemini, Firebase, AI Studio, or Google Cloud.
- Teams that want a more visual supervisory workflow than a terminal-only agent.

## Who They Miss

- Users skeptical of Google's product churn across Jules, Gemini CLI, AI Studio, and Antigravity.
- Teams that need local-first, narrow-scope browser authority rather than a full IDE with broad terminal/filesystem access.
- Buyers who require transparent cost, raw trace logs, and strict permission evidence before trusting autonomous agents.

## Agent Browser Opportunity

- Adopt the artifact lesson but make the browser evidence more inspectable: exact action trace, screenshots, recordings, DOM state, approvals, and command boundaries.
- Stay narrower than a whole IDE so the authority model is easier to explain.
- Make verification artifacts parseable enough for tests, PRs, and audits rather than just attractive review cards.
