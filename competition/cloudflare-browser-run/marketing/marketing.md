# Cloudflare Browser Run Marketing

## Audience

- Developers already building on Cloudflare Workers.
- AI-agent builders who need browser-based automation where APIs do not exist.
- Teams generating screenshots, PDFs, markdown, crawled content, links, and dynamic-page extracts.
- Compliance-minded organizations that prefer signed, well-behaved automation over stealth scraping.

## Positioning

- Core claim: headless programmable browsers built for AI agents, running at scale on Cloudflare's global network.
- Competitive frame: browser automation as a native serverless primitive, connected to the rest of Cloudflare's compute, AI, storage, and security portfolio.
- Design contrast: Cloudflare emphasizes ethical/compliant bot identification, while many scraping vendors emphasize CAPTCHA and bot-defense bypass.

## Customer Model

- Available on Workers Free and Paid plans.
- Free plan includes daily browser usage with lower concurrency and request limits.
- Paid plan includes monthly browser hours and averaged concurrent-browser allowance, then charges additional browser-hour and concurrency usage.
- Enterprise growth path is naturally tied to broader Cloudflare platform procurement.

## Who They Capture

- Existing Cloudflare customers who want browser automation without another vendor.
- Agent developers who need cheap browser hours and markdown/screenshot primitives.
- Teams that value network-edge deployment, Workers bindings, and platform integration more than a polished browser-agent UI.

## Who They Miss

- Buyers that need stealth-first access to heavily defended public sites.
- Local-first users who need the agent to operate inside their existing logged-in browser.
- Teams that need built-in session replay, approval flows, and evidence review rather than infrastructure APIs.
