# dassi Marketing

## Audience

- Knowledge workers who live in Gmail, Slack, Notion, LinkedIn, Salesforce, HubSpot, Sheets, docs, and forms.
- Users who want browser automation but do not want to configure MCP servers, debug ports, or cloud browser sessions.
- Privacy-sensitive users who prefer BYOK or direct routing to model providers.

## Positioning

- Core claim: an AI browser agent that works where the user works.
- Category frame: AI coworker in the browser, not a separate chatbot.
- Trust frame: local browser context, user approvals, no vendor access to browsing data, and user choice of Claude/GPT/Gemini/Llama-style providers.

## Customer Model

- Chrome extension install with a 14-day free trial.
- Managed credits for low-friction onboarding and BYOK for users who already have model access.
- Potential expansion into paid seats for saved skills, scheduling, and higher usage.

## Who They Capture

- Nontechnical users who want practical browser assistance now.
- Users who already trust Chrome extensions and sidebars.
- Workers whose tasks are repetitive but too custom for Zapier-style API automations.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who need programmatic browser APIs, MCP logs, screenshots, and test/eval artifacts.
- Enterprises requiring centralized policy and audit controls.
- Users who want a full AI browser replacement rather than an extension.
