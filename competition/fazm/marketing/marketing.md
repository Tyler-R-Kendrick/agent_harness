# Fazm Marketing

## Audience

- Mac users who want voice-controlled automation across the whole desktop.
- Small businesses and teams doing repetitive data entry, CRM, support, operations, and documentation work.
- Enterprises that like RPA outcomes but dislike cloud screen capture, long setup cycles, and brittle visual scripting.
- Developers and operators who want an open-source local computer-use agent.

## Positioning

Fazm positions itself as a local-first AI computer agent. The consumer pitch is speed and voice control; the team pitch is reusable workflows with admin controls and auditability; the enterprise pitch is screen data staying on device.

## Customer Model

- Free-to-start and open-source desktop distribution through GitHub.
- Enterprise pilot and team deployment motion with shared workflow libraries, policy controls, SSO, audit logs, and on-prem options.
- Likely captures both individual power users and teams with repetitive app workflows that are too small or fast-moving for traditional RPA.

## Who It Captures

- Users who want one agent for browser, apps, files, and documents.
- Teams that need local screen privacy and audit logs.
- Operators who prefer natural-language workflow creation to RPA scripting.
- Mac-first organizations where ScreenCaptureKit and Accessibility are acceptable dependencies.

## Who It Does Not Capture

- Windows-heavy or browser-only organizations that do not want a Mac desktop agent.
- Buyers that require mature managed governance before allowing screen and file control.
- Workflows where voice input is impractical or too ambiguous.
- Developers who need browser-specific DOM traces, screenshots, network logs, and deterministic test artifacts rather than general desktop control.

## Competitive Takeaway

Fazm raises expectations for `agent-browser` around local privacy, audit logs, and team workflow reuse. The browser-specific answer should be richer page evidence and narrower authority rather than trying to control every app.
