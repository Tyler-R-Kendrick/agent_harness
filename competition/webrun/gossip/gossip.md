# WebRun Gossip

## Positive Signals

- First-party copy is unusually specific about integration paths: REST, WebSocket, MCP, OpenAI-compatible API, Telegram, Zapier, LangChain, Clay, n8n, Anthropic, Make, CrewAI, and Vercel AI SDK.
- The docs explain when to use REST, WebSocket, OpenAI-compatible APIs, or MCP, which lowers integration ambiguity.
- The product visibly attacks the two pain points buyers complain about most in browser automation: selector fragility and slow cold starts.

## Negative Signals

- Public beta status means independent production reliability signals are limited.
- Searchable public issue/community chatter is sparse, so the main evidence is controlled first-party material.
- The strongest claims, including sub-100ms decisioning and very low average task cost, need real customer proof before they should be treated as durable advantages.

## Bug And UX Risk Themes

- Fast visual action can still be wrong; the UX must make verification, replay, and rollback easy.
- Desktop environments introduce file, credential, clipboard, and app-level risks that browser-only products avoid.
- Policy controls can become hard to manage if teams need many domain/action/sensitive-data rules across workflows.
