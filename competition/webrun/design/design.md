# WebRun Design

## Look And Feel

- The site is a high-contrast developer infrastructure landing page with animated workflow demos, task timelines, code snippets, and pricing panels.
- Visual examples emphasize real work: PDFs, BLS downloads, Google Drive uploads, LinkedIn, HubSpot, Gmail, Google Sheets, filesystem cards, and policy guardrail overlays.
- The UI vocabulary is operational rather than whimsical: sessions, environments, policies, live viewing, structured results, REST, MCP, WebSocket.

## Design Tokens To Track

```yaml
surface: dark infrastructure SaaS with demo panels
accent: bright blue/green success states and warning panels
primary_control: API/MCP task request
secondary_controls:
  - live session viewer
  - policy approval
  - persistent environment selector
  - file storage
  - integration tabs
trust_controls:
  - human-in-the-loop
  - policy guardrails
  - immutable audit trail
  - live video streaming
information_density: high
```

## Differentiators

- Sells a complete desktop environment, not just a browser tab.
- Claims sub-100ms decisions, 800ms first action, about eight seconds prompt-to-result, and CDP fallback for edge cases.
- Combines fast vision execution with policy controls and multiple integration protocols.

## What Is Good

- The speed positioning is crisp and easy to compare against slow queue-based browser farms.
- Policy demos make safety concrete: domain rules, action rules, sensitive-data detection, compliance templates, and approval gates.
- The persistent environment story is useful for multi-step workflows that need cookies, files, memory, and scheduled tasks.

## Where It Breaks Down

- The page is claim-heavy and benchmark-light; buyers will want reproducible evals and reliability data beyond marketing numbers.
- Full desktop access broadens the security surface beyond browser-specific controls.
- Token-based pricing for browser actions may be harder for operations teams to predict than per-minute or per-session pricing.
