# WebRun Marketing

## Audience

- Developers building browser-using AI agents.
- Operations teams automating lead enrichment, insurance verification, recruiting, CRM, document transfer, and other workflows that lack stable APIs.
- Teams using n8n, Make, Clay, Zapier, LangChain, OpenAI, Anthropic, CrewAI, or Vercel AI SDK.

## Positioning

- Core claim: fastest browsing agent and desktop automation platform.
- Category frame: browser automation infrastructure for AI agents, not no-code RPA or traditional Playwright/Selenium.
- Differentiator frame: AI-first vision engine, verification, CDP fallback, persistent desktops, and policy controls.

## Customer Model

- Public beta with free trial.
- Pay-as-you-go pricing advertised around token rates and an average task cost.
- Volume discounts and demo booking suggest an enterprise/usage expansion path.

## Who They Capture

- AI engineers who want an MCP/API endpoint rather than owning browser fleets.
- Automation teams frustrated by selector maintenance.
- Buyers who need live session viewing, audit trails, and policy controls before granting agents real web access.

## Who They Miss

- Users who want local-first execution in their own browser profile.
- Buyers who distrust first-party performance claims without published third-party evals.
- Teams with predictable per-seat procurement expectations rather than variable usage pricing.
