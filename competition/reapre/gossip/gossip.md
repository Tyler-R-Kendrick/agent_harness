# Reapre Gossip

## Public sentiment

- Direct third-party chatter for Reapre is sparse as of this snapshot. The product should be treated as an early or low-public-footprint entrant rather than a market-proven default.
- Broader automation discussions repeatedly warn that AI automation fails when teams automate before understanding the process, lack feedback signals, or expect "set and forget" autonomy.
- Reapre's own positioning addresses some of that critique with recordings, approvals, retries, webhooks, and replay bundles, but the public proof surface is still thin.

## Design and UX complaints to watch

- One-control-plane positioning can become overwhelming if the run UI does not make the current target, permission scope, cost, and evidence obvious.
- Device/app breadth creates a larger blast radius than browser-only tooling; buyers will ask what an agent can touch and how quickly it can be stopped.
- Request-volume pricing is simpler than per-step agent billing, but users may still need per-workflow cost estimation before long-running jobs.
