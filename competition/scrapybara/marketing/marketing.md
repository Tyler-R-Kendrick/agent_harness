# Scrapybara Marketing

## Audience

- Developers building computer-use agents with OpenAI CUA, Claude Computer Use, or similar models.
- Teams that need remote desktops with browser, filesystem, code execution, and environment control.
- AI automation builders who want to scale many isolated instances quickly.

## Positioning

- Scrapybara positions itself as "a computer for your AI."
- The pitch is not merely scraping or browser automation; it is remote infrastructure for free-form computer-use workflows.
- The marketing emphasizes speed, scale, stability, interactive streams, authenticated access, and session persistence.

## Customer Model

- Developer-infrastructure usage model around instance types, compute cost, and agent credits.
- Likely captures teams who already accept cloud execution and want less operational burden than managing VMs, browsers, VNC, and model tool loops.

## Who They Capture

- Computer-use agent builders who need Ubuntu, Browser, or Windows environments.
- Teams running batch research, scraping, QA, or operational tasks where a full desktop is useful.
- Developers who want SDK-level access to model/tool loops rather than building orchestration from scratch.

## Who They Miss

- Users who want a visible daily browser app with local history, approvals, and inspectable workspace state.
- Teams that cannot route logged-in sessions through a hosted desktop vendor.
- Developers who only need a cheap CDP browser endpoint.
