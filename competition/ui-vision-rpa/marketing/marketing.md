# Ui.Vision RPA Marketing

## Audience

- RPA practitioners, QA engineers, power users, and iMacros/Selenium IDE users who want browser macros with visual fallback.
- Local-first automation users who prefer open-source tooling and do not want every screenshot or OCR action sent to a cloud service.
- Teams that need to automate legacy desktops, Citrix-style surfaces, PDFs, screenshots, or applications without accessible DOM structures.

## Positioning

- Ui.Vision frames itself as open-source RPA for browser, desktop, screen scraping, UI testing, and test automation.
- The product claims a hybrid model: Selenium-style commands plus computer vision/OCR plus native XModules.
- The design pitch is pragmatic rather than magical: record macros, run demo macros, inspect commands, and add visual "eyes" when selectors are not enough.

## Customer Model

- Free extension adoption across Chrome, Edge, and Firefox.
- Paid/pro path appears around XModules, enterprise needs, update control, support, and OCR server capabilities.
- Enterprise story is less polished than modern SaaS competitors, but the open-source/local angle can satisfy buyers who distrust hosted browser agents.

## Captures

- Users with long-lived macro investments or Selenium IDE mental models.
- QA and RPA users who need local screenshots, OCR, desktop automation, and command-line runs.
- Workflows where the browser is only one part of a broader desktop automation sequence.

## Misses

- Users who expect a conversational agent to plan and recover autonomously.
- Teams that want modern collaboration, trace dashboards, and approval UX.
- Buyers who are unwilling to install native modules or learn a command-heavy macro language.
