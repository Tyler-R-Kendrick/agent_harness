# Lindy Marketing

## Audience

- Professionals who want an AI assistant for email, meetings, calendar, and follow-ups.
- Small businesses and operators that need no-code workflow automation.
- Teams willing to pay for an agent configured for them rather than building one from scratch.

## Positioning

- Lindy is positioned as an AI assistant that runs work life, not as a developer tool.
- The no-code agent-builder message emphasizes speed: powerful agents without engineering effort, with custom agents launchable quickly.
- Integrations are a central trust cue, placing Lindy near Zapier, Make, and n8n in the buyer's mental model while adding AI decision-making.

## Customer Model

- Plus, Pro, Max, and Enterprise plans with connected inbox limits and usage tiers.
- Computer use appears in Pro and above, making browser-like control a premium capability.
- Enterprise and services-led workflows target teams with compliance or production-readiness needs.

## Who They Capture

- Busy professionals and business owners who value saved time over technical control.
- Teams whose core work is email/calendar/CRM/sales/support coordination.
- Buyers who want AI automation but do not want to manage browser sessions, Playwright, or MCP tools.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who need local browser traces, screenshots, branch/session artifacts, and extensible runtime internals.
- Users who want predictable flat-rate automation costs.
- Teams that require detailed step-level evidence for each web action.
