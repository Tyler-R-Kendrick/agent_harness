# Lindy Gossip

## Positive Signals

- Users and reviewers praise how easy it can be to build useful outreach or workflow agents compared with more technical automation tools.
- The integration catalog and office-assistant framing make the product understandable to non-engineers.

## Negative Signals

- Trustpilot reviews include complaints about high pricing, lag, poor support channels, confusing setup, credit limits, and failures to access some public websites or services.
- Community support posts describe repeated workflow failures where an agent step does not produce the expected output before moving on.
- Reddit discussion says Lindy can be easier than raw automation tools, but may not give enough low-level configuration control for precise workflows.

## Product Risks To Watch

- If computer use is used as a fallback for missing integrations, failures may be harder to debug than connector failures.
- Credit-based pricing can make users conservative about experimentation, which slows workflow tuning.
- Assistant branding raises expectations that the product should work reliably across messy personal work contexts, not just curated demos.
