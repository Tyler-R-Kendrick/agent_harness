# Lindy Design

## Look And Feel

- Lindy is marketed as an assistant for the user's work life, with a workflow-builder feel rather than a browser-first surface.
- Product language centers on inboxes, meetings, calendar, follow-ups, integrations, and agents built from plain English.
- The design presents breadth through integration catalogs and packaged work outcomes, including computer use on higher tiers.

## Differentiators

- Lindy competes through office workflow ownership rather than browser automation alone.
- Hundreds of integrations make it feel closer to an AI-native Zapier/assistant hybrid.
- The "built in 48 hours" service motion helps non-technical businesses buy outcomes instead of configuring agents themselves.

## What Is Good

- Packaging around email, meetings, calendar, and follow-ups gives users concrete recurring jobs to delegate.
- Plain-language agent creation is easier for business users than API-first browser-agent platforms.
- Computer use extends Lindy beyond pure API integrations for web-app tasks that do not have a clean connector.

## Where It Breaks Down

- Workflow-builder surfaces can become confusing when users need raw configuration precision.
- Credit-based usage creates monitoring overhead, especially for high-volume or long-running automations.
- Public complaints mention lag, fast credit burn, website access/IP block failures, and unintuitive setup.
