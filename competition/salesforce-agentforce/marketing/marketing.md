# Salesforce Agentforce Marketing

## Audience

- Salesforce customers in sales, service, marketing, commerce, contact centers, and Slack-heavy organizations.
- CIOs and CRM leaders who want AI agents governed by the same data, metadata, and workflow model as their Salesforce estate.
- ISVs and consultants who can package agents, tools, and implementation services for AgentExchange.

## Positioning

- Salesforce sells Agentforce as "digital labor" and the agentic layer of the Salesforce Platform.
- The product is positioned around trusted enterprise outcomes, not generic chat: CRM grounding, Data 360, metadata, Atlas reasoning, Trust Layer, topics, actions, and audit.
- AgentExchange turns ecosystem depth into a marketing advantage by combining apps, agents, sub-agents, Slack extensions, tools, and MCP servers.

## Customer Model

- Existing Salesforce customers expand through Agentforce 360 capabilities, Data 360, packaged service/sales agents, Flex Credits, and partner implementation.
- Agentforce monetization is consumption-oriented, with conversation or action/Flex Credit models layered onto existing Salesforce licenses and data products.
- Partner and ISV motions are central: Salesforce wants a trusted marketplace that makes agents discoverable and purchasable through the existing Salesforce buying channel.

## Who They Capture

- Enterprises whose workflows and customer records are already in Salesforce.
- Contact center and service teams that need AI handoff, transcripts, escalation, and CRM-native action.
- Salesforce partners that want a marketplace and certification path for AI agent offerings.

## Who They Miss

- Teams that need browser automation outside CRM systems, local session evidence, or page-level replay.
- Builders who want cheaper model/API choice without Salesforce platform, Data 360, or credit-metering overhead.
- Smaller teams intimidated by Salesforce setup, product naming, and partner implementation complexity.
