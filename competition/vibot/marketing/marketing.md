# Vibot Marketing

## Audience

- Developers and teams that want self-hosted browser automation rather than per-run cloud infrastructure.
- Operators replacing ad hoc Puppeteer/Playwright scripts, cron jobs, and monitoring checks.
- Agent builders who want MCP routing, local LLM support, multi-agent orchestration, and a dashboard.
- Data teams extracting structured website data with pagination, backoff, and export.

## Positioning

- Core claim: AI-native self-hosted browser automation with visual workflows, multi-agent orchestration, and resilient execution.
- Competitive frame: more complete than Browserbase, Skyvern, Browser Use, and Playwright MCP because it combines builder, recorder, CLI, MCP, local LLMs, and monitoring.
- Trust frame: MIT licensed, self-hosted, no API keys required to start, and data sovereignty.

## Customer Model

- Free MIT open-source distribution.
- No hosted or enterprise pricing visible at snapshot time.
- Adoption is likely through GitHub, developer docs, and self-hosted experimentation.

## Who They Capture

- Teams that want ownership and do not want hosted per-run browser pricing.
- Developers who need more than an MCP server but less than a large RPA suite.
- Users who want visual workflows plus code/CLI escape hatches.

## Who They Miss

- Non-technical users who do not want to clone a repo or operate a local server.
- Enterprises needing commercial support, SLAs, admin policy, and centralized audit.
- Teams that prefer managed browser fleets with proxy, CAPTCHA, identity, and trace retention handled for them.
