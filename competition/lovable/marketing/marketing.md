# Lovable Marketing

## Audience

- Non-technical founders, operators, product managers, designers, marketers, and small teams that want a working web app without hiring an engineering team first.
- Developers who want a fast visual baseline and are willing to take over code later.
- Agencies and internal teams that need quick prototypes, customer portals, landing pages, dashboards, and SaaS MVPs.

## Positioning

- Lovable positions itself around speed from idea to product: prompt an app, refine it visually, connect backend services, and publish.
- The product message is less "AI coding assistant" and more "AI-native app builder" with no-code accessibility and code escape hatches.
- Supabase, GitHub, Stripe, and publishing integrations make the platform feel like a practical product launcher rather than only a UI generator.

## Customer Model

- Free usage provides a small daily/monthly credit allowance and private projects.
- Paid plans add larger credit pools, custom domains, roles and permissions, collaboration, and business/enterprise controls.
- Users may also carry separate costs for Supabase, Stripe, third-party APIs, domains, and production hosting choices.

## Who They Capture

- Builders who care more about fast visible progress than local development control.
- Teams that want designers and operators to make UI changes without opening an IDE.
- MVP buyers who are comfortable with web-app defaults and integrated backend services.

## Who They Miss

- Engineering teams that require deterministic tests, code review gates, staging environments, migrations, and security evidence before publish.
- Buyers building native mobile apps or complex offline/client-device workflows.
- Cost-sensitive users who do not want debugging to consume the same credits as feature work.
