# Dia Marketing

## Audience

- Arc users and design-forward browser enthusiasts.
- Knowledge workers who use ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity but dislike switching tabs.
- Students and writers who benefit from tab-aware study and drafting workflows.

## Positioning

- Market frame: AI should be baked into the browser where work already happens.
- Differentiator: less radical than Arc, more AI-native than Chrome.
- Product promise: chat with tabs, use history as context, create Skills, and personalize the assistant.

## Customer Model

- Free browser access with limited AI usage.
- Pro tier around $20/month for heavier AI chat/skills users.
- Atlassian acquisition suggests future enterprise/team distribution potential.

## Who They Capture

- People who wanted Arc's craft but not Arc's complexity.
- Mac users willing to try a new primary browser.
- Users who want AI as a writing/research companion rather than a fully autonomous operator.

## Who They Miss

- Windows/Linux users until platform support expands.
- Security-sensitive organizations without mature admin controls.
- Developers needing programmable browser-agent infrastructure.
