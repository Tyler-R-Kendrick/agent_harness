# Runner H / Surfer H Marketing

## Audience

- Developers and QA teams maintaining brittle selector-based web automations.
- Business teams that want one-prompt workflows across SaaS apps, documents, calendars, messaging, and live web data.
- Enterprises exploring specialized computer-use models rather than generic LLM-controlled browser wrappers.

## Positioning

- Core claim: chatbots answer; Runner H executes.
- Technical claim: specialized Holo action VLMs can drive web interfaces more cheaply and accurately than large generalist models.
- Product claim: Studio turns web automation into production workflows with review, editing, debugging, and teaching surfaces.

## Customer Model

- Private/beta access and "talk to an expert" sales flow for business users.
- Portal API keys and CLI/open-weight distribution for technical adoption.
- Enterprise expansion likely comes from app integrations, security, model deployment, training on company traces, and process automation.

## Who They Capture

- Buyers who believe browser automation needs purpose-built action models.
- Teams that want web browsing as one agent inside broader business workflow orchestration.
- Developers attracted to open weights, CLI control, and benchmark claims.

## Who They Miss

- Users who want a stable, generally available browser-agent workbench today.
- Teams that need simple product boundaries and clear packaging.
- Local-first users who prioritize browser profile ownership, auditable approvals, and trace artifacts over model benchmark wins.

## Competitive Read

H pressures `agent-browser` by making high-quality action prediction and self-healing web workflows feel like model infrastructure. `agent-browser` should counter with a simpler inspectable product surface: run history, approvals, screenshots, traces, and deterministic recovery controls around local browser authority.
