# Runner H / Surfer H Gossip

## Positive Signals

- H's public pages emphasize strong WebVoyager and UI-action benchmark results, including Surfer H accuracy claims and specialized VLM performance.
- Open Surfer-H CLI and model-weight distribution give developers a path to inspect and experiment instead of only watching demos.
- The product message lands on a real pain: selector maintenance, UI changes, and long-running browser workflows are expensive.

## Negative Signals

- Public availability is uneven: Runner H is described in beta/private-beta terms in some surfaces while newer pages present broader business workflows.
- Benchmark claims are not a substitute for production evidence on authenticated, messy, high-stakes websites.
- The product line can be hard to explain because Runner H, Surfer H, Studio, Holo models, API keys, app integrations, and payments are all in the same brand field.

## Bug And UX Risk Themes

- A VLM that clicks accurately can still choose the wrong business step without strong planning, source credibility, and stop/retry rules.
- Studio review/editing must make wrong turns obvious; otherwise "self-healing" can hide false positives.
- Autonomous payments and app integrations raise approval, spending, and data-retention questions.
