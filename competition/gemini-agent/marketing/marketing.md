# Gemini Agent Marketing

## Audience

- Google users who already rely on Chrome, Search, Gmail, Docs, Calendar, Android, and Workspace.
- Consumers and professionals who want AI help inside daily Google surfaces.
- Paid Gemini plan customers who expect more autonomous work from the assistant.

## Positioning

- Core frame: Gemini becomes a universal Google assistant that can reason, see, and act.
- Project Mariner positions browser agents as a human-agent interaction frontier.
- Google markets the advantage as useful context across the tools people already use.

## Customer Model

- Bundled ecosystem distribution through Google accounts and Gemini apps.
- Premium access and higher limits through paid Google AI plans.
- Enterprise capture through Google Workspace and admin controls.

## Who They Capture

- Mainstream Chrome and Google Workspace users.
- Users who prefer AI features bundled into tools they already trust.
- Buyers who want vendor consolidation around Google identity and data.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who need open traces, MCP-style extension points, and local test fixtures.
- Users who avoid Google account centralization.
- Teams whose workflows span non-Google identity, non-Chrome browsers, or self-hosted environments.
