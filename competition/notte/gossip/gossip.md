# Notte Gossip

## Positive Signals

- Public marketing claims trust from 100+ AI companies and partners and highlights Cloudflare, Fingerprint, Fetch.ai, and Massive logos.
- The Notte site features builder testimonials praising speed, reliability, and design polish.
- GitHub organization messaging reinforces the same wedge: a perception layer that turns pages into an action API and lowers LLM cognitive load.

## Negative Signals

- Many public claims are vendor-provided and benchmark-oriented, so they need independent verification before product decisions.
- The pricing story is aggressive but proxy-heavy workflows can still become expensive because proxy traffic dominates the example cost.
- The platform overlaps with many competitors at once: Browserbase, Browserless, Browser Use, Skyvern, scraping APIs, serverless functions, and auth platforms.

## Bug And UX Risk Themes

- "Use agents or convert to functions" is compelling, but the conversion path must preserve correctness and failure handling.
- Vaults and identities are trust-critical; any ambiguity around who can access secrets would damage the core product promise.
- The Studio and Demonstrate Mode surfaces need excellent design or they risk becoming another brittle recorder/low-code automation tool.
