# Devin Marketing

## Audience

- Engineering leaders with backlogs of well-scoped implementation, migration, modernization, and review work.
- Platform and enterprise teams that need AI coding work to flow through GitHub, GitLab, Bitbucket, Azure DevOps, Jira, Linear, Slack, Teams, and APIs.
- Organizations willing to buy a managed agent worker instead of assembling a local agent stack.

## Positioning

- "AI software engineer" rather than pair programmer.
- The product promises delegated work: sessions, code changes, PRs, review loops, and enterprise administration.
- Release-note emphasis on review, wiki, guardrails, MCP marketplace, audit logs, and consumption APIs positions Devin as an engineering operations product, not only a coding chat app.

## Customer Model

- Self-serve has Free, Pro, Max, and Teams plans with included quota plus on-demand credits.
- Enterprise customers are billed through Agent Compute Units set by order form.
- The enterprise wedge is governance: SSO/security controls, auditability, cost reporting, integration administration, and session caps.

## Who They Capture

- Managers who want to assign tasks and review PRs later.
- Enterprises that need a vendor-owned autonomous agent with admin surfaces.
- Teams with enough review bandwidth to turn agent output into shipped work.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who want local-first inspectability, custom runtime policy, or browser-specific trace artifacts.
- Cost-sensitive individual builders who compare agent spend against flat-rate model subscriptions.
- Teams that need deterministic browser evidence rather than an autonomous code worker.
