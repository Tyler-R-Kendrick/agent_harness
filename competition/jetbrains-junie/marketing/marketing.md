# JetBrains Junie Marketing

## Who They Are Marketing To

- Existing IntelliJ IDEA, PyCharm, WebStorm, GoLand, Rider, and JetBrains IDE users.
- Teams that trust JetBrains project intelligence and want AI assistance without abandoning their IDE.
- Developers who want model choice across OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Grok, BYOK, local Ollama, and custom providers.
- Organizations that want AI features tied to JetBrains IDE licensing and IDE Services governance.

## Positioning

- Junie is positioned as JetBrains' AI coding agent: it autonomously plans and executes complex multi-step actions from prompts.
- The CLI is positioned as LLM-agnostic and barrier-free, usable in terminal, any IDE, CI/CD, and GitHub/GitLab workflows.
- JetBrains frames Junie as context-aware by default because it combines LLMs with JetBrains project understanding.
- The public site emphasizes persistent guidelines, agent skills, custom subagents, MCP integration, ACP, and BYOK.

## Customer Model

- JetBrains AI has Free, Pro, Ultimate, and Enterprise tiers.
- AI Pro includes a smaller quota and top-ups; AI Ultimate is recommended for regular Junie use with a higher credit quota.
- Junie Free to Start supports BYOK with provider-rate pricing and no markup, plus local models.
- Commercial pricing is higher than individual pricing, and Enterprise is tied to JetBrains IDE Services.

## Who They Capture

- JetBrains-heavy shops where IDE integration, debugger context, language support, and project indexing matter more than editor novelty.
- Developers who want to bring a coding agent into existing terminals or ACP clients while retaining JetBrains semantics.
- Buyers who prefer a known developer-tools vendor over newer AI-agent startups.

## Who They Do Not Capture

- VS Code-first developers who prefer Cursor, Windsurf, Copilot, or lightweight CLI agents.
- Windows developers who rely on WSL for their main development environment.
- Users who need browser traces, screenshots, video artifacts, tab state, or authenticated web workflow evidence.
- Budget-sensitive users who want simple unlimited-feeling agent usage.

## Competitive Note For agent-browser

Junie raises the bar for project context, review mode, debug mode, and MCP setup. `agent-browser` should borrow the explicit project-scope and plan/review patterns, but differentiate with browser-native evidence, visual artifacts, and live authority controls.
