# Octomind Marketing

## Audience

- SaaS, e-commerce, and agency teams with web apps and brittle E2E test suites.
- QA leaders who need lower flake rates, faster debugging, and CI-ready browser coverage.
- Product and engineering teams that want non-specialists to create test cases visually.

## Positioning

- Core claim: stable, scalable E2E tests from day one.
- Competitive frame: generic coding agents only see code, while Octomind sees browser execution traces, DOM snapshots, and network logs.
- Buyer promise: faster test creation, stable execution, instant debugging, and self-healing maintenance.

## Customer Model

- Subscription plans are organized around test cases, cloud runs, parallel executions, projects/URLs, AI test creations, and maximum steps.
- Enterprise plans expand into custom volume, support, project limits, compliance, and integration needs.
- The wedge is operational value: fewer flaky tests, clearer CI failures, and less manual maintenance.

## Who They Capture

- Teams with enough deployment volume that flaky E2E tests are already painful.
- QA organizations that want browser evidence, traces, and dashboards more than raw browser automation APIs.
- Buyers that need SOC2, CI/CD, private-worker access, and test-management integrations.

## Who They Miss

- General-purpose browser-agent users who want research, shopping, data entry, or local personal browsing.
- Developers who want only a lightweight Playwright/MCP tool inside their existing agent loop.
- Teams skeptical that AI selector healing can preserve business intent.

## Competitive Read

Octomind is a targeted competitor to `agent-browser`'s visual proof and runtime trace story. It validates that browser execution evidence is valuable, but keeps that value inside QA. `agent-browser` can generalize the same evidence model across non-test browser work.
