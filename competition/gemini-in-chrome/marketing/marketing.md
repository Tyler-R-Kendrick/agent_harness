# Gemini In Chrome Marketing

## Audience

- Mainstream Chrome users in supported regions who want page summaries, tab comparison, and quick answers without switching tools.
- Google AI Pro/Ultra subscribers who want early access to agentic browsing.
- Users already invested in Google apps and Chrome sync.

## Positioning

- Core claim: AI assistance, right in your browser.
- Market frame: Chrome can preserve normal browsing while adding contextual Gemini help, history recall, app-aware answers, and autonomous task previews.
- Product promise: summarize pages, compare tabs, work with Google apps, rediscover history, and automate routine tasks while keeping the user in control.
- AI Pointer promise: point at the part of a webpage that matters, speak or type compact shorthand, and let Gemini infer the target, entities, and next action.

## Customer Model

- Bundled into Chrome through staged rollout for eligible users.
- Advanced/preview agentic features are tied first to Google AI Pro and Ultra subscription access.
- Enterprise and school access depends on admin controls, so rollout is not uniform.
- AI Pointer is being integrated into Gemini in Chrome, with experimental Google AI Studio demos for image editing and map/place lookup and a Magic Pointer rollout planned for Googlebook.

## Who They Capture

- Existing Chrome users who will not install a new AI browser.
- Users who trust Google account context and want help across Gmail, Calendar, Maps, YouTube, and browsing.
- Casual users who need summaries and comparisons more than full autonomous agents.
- Users who struggle to translate visual intent into prompts: shoppers comparing selected products, people editing images, readers summarizing PDFs, and users extracting places or tasks from visual content.

## Who They Miss

- Users outside supported regions, languages, or account types.
- Privacy-first users uncomfortable with browser history, page content, and Google app context flowing into AI.
- Developers who want inspectable traces, local-first models, or programmable automation surfaces.
- Users who need explicit audit artifacts showing the exact pointer region, extracted entity, model prompt, and confirmation boundary.
