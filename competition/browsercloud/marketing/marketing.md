# BrowserCloud Marketing

## Audience

- AI-app builders, scraping teams, QA teams, and automation developers.
- Buyers replacing self-managed Chromium fleets.
- Teams that need predictable concurrency and proxy capacity more than a full no-code agent surface.

## Positioning

- Core claim: browser automation for AI apps.
- Market frame: developers need scalable browser infrastructure with stealth, CAPTCHA handling, monitoring, and integrations.
- Product promise: start free, then scale browser hours, HTTP API calls, proxies, and concurrency as workloads grow.

## Customer Model

- Free-to-paid SaaS with credit pools.
- Low-end self-serve plans for hobby/startup usage.
- Business and enterprise tiers for high concurrency, dedicated clusters, improved stealth proxies, security controls, and SLAs.

## Who They Capture

- Cost-sensitive developers comparing cloud browser plans.
- Web-data and testing teams that already know Playwright/Puppeteer-style automation.
- Workflow automation users who want n8n, Zapier, or Make integrations.

## Who They Miss

- Local-first users who want their own authenticated browser profile.
- Agent-workbench users who need explicit action review, memory, approvals, and replayable reasoning.
- Enterprises looking for a detailed governance story before allowing AI-controlled browsing.
