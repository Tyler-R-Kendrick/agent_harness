# BrowserMCP Marketing

## Audience

- Developers and power users who already use MCP-compatible coding assistants.
- Users who want AI automation over websites where they are already logged in.
- Privacy-sensitive builders who do not want a cloud browser vendor in the request path.

## Positioning

- "Automate your browser with AI" is the plain-language pitch.
- Differentiates from Playwright MCP by controlling the user's real browser instead of starting a new automation browser.
- Differentiates from cloud browser APIs by emphasizing local performance, privacy, logged-in state, and real fingerprints.

## Customer Model

- Current visible model is open-source/free distribution through GitHub, NPM/MCP directories, and Chrome extension adoption.
- The strongest acquisition channel is developer virality inside Claude, Cursor, Windsurf, VS Code, and MCP directories.
- Potential paid expansion would likely be team policy, audit, hosted relay, enterprise packaging, or premium extension capabilities.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who need quick browser access inside an assistant without writing Playwright scripts.
- Users automating SaaS tasks where login and 2FA setup would make a headless session painful.
- Builders who see "local" as safer than cloud sessions.

## Who They Miss

- Teams needing managed browser fleets, replays, proxies, scale, compliance, and deterministic CI artifacts.
- Non-technical users who need a polished app workspace rather than extension/server setup.
- Organizations requiring strong trace governance, approvals, and auditable run history.
