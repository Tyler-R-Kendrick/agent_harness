# Rove Marketing

## Audience

- Developers building browser-using AI agents who do not want to operate Playwright fleets.
- Teams worried about screenshot and full-DOM token cost.
- Users of Claude, Cursor, VS Code, or MCP clients that need a hosted browser tool quickly.
- Builders of agent research, form filling, visual regression, and competitive-monitoring workflows.

## Positioning

- Core claim: browser automation for the agentic web with accessibility-tree output instead of expensive screenshot loops.
- Competitive frame: a hosted API with zero infrastructure, MCP-native integration, Playwright semantics, warm contexts, and recorded sessions.
- Cost frame: the landing page translates perception choices into token and dollar deltas.

## Customer Model

- Free signup credits.
- Early-access Founder Packs sold as one-time credit bundles.
- Pay-as-you-go is mentioned as the cheapest per-credit option, but the visible page emphasizes Founder Packs.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who like Playwright but do not want to manage browsers, queues, or warm pools.
- Cost-sensitive teams whose agents are bottlenecked by large page observations.
- Teams that need replayable video evidence without building their own recorder.

## Who They Miss

- Local-first users who do not want remote browser sessions touching authenticated work.
- Enterprises needing long artifact retention, custom compliance, and private networking details before adoption.
- Users who need a full visual operator console rather than an API and MCP server.
