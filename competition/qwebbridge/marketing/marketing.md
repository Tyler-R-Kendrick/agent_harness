# QWebBridge Marketing

## Audience

- Developers using Claude Code, Kimi Code, Cursor, Codex, Hermes, OpenClaw, or custom agents.
- Users who want agents to operate an already-authenticated local Chrome session.
- Teams that prefer open-source localhost bridges over remote browser sessions.

## Positioning

QWebBridge positions itself as an open-source, self-hosted browser bridge for AI agents. The copy leans on real tabs, real sessions, Kimi WebBridge compatibility, and multiple protocol options.

## Customer Model

- Open-source distribution through CLI, Chrome extension, and GitHub.
- No obvious paid tier in the current snapshot.
- Adoption is likely driven by agent-client ecosystems and copyable setup snippets.

## Capture

- Captures developers who need local browser access inside coding agents.
- Captures users who distrust cloud browsers for cookies, login sessions, and page content.
- Captures existing Kimi WebBridge users who want a self-hosted or MCP-compatible path.

## Miss

- Misses nontechnical users who do not want to run a daemon or load extensions.
- Misses enterprise buyers who need admin policy, fleet management, audit exports, and support contracts.
- Misses workflows where remote concurrency, proxies, CAPTCHA support, and managed identity are more important than local privacy.
