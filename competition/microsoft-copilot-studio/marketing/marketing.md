# Microsoft Copilot Studio Marketing

## Audience

- Microsoft 365 customers that want agents inside Teams, Copilot Chat, Outlook, SharePoint, and Office workflows.
- Power Platform makers and IT admins responsible for app connectors, environments, governance, and business process automation.
- Enterprises that prefer permission-aware agents grounded in Microsoft Graph and existing compliance controls.

## Positioning

- "Build agents, your way" across Copilot Studio, Microsoft 365 Agent Builder, Agents Toolkit, Azure AI Foundry, and GitHub.
- Microsoft positions agents as a natural extension of Microsoft 365 Copilot rather than a separate browser or automation product.
- The selling motion emphasizes secure enterprise grounding, admin controls, connectors, prebuilt tools, and productivity inside daily work apps.

## Customer Model

- Employee-facing scenarios benefit from Microsoft 365 Copilot licensing when the agent runs for authenticated licensed users under their identity.
- Full Copilot Studio supports broader publishing and paid consumption, especially for external channels, autonomous triggers, generative tools, and Power Platform integrations.
- The commercial model expands existing Microsoft 365 and Power Platform customers rather than asking them to adopt a new standalone agent vendor.

## Who They Capture

- CIO-led organizations already standardizing on Microsoft 365 Copilot.
- Department teams that want quick internal agents over SharePoint, Teams, files, and business apps.
- Admins who need governance through familiar Microsoft tenant, identity, and compliance controls.

## Who They Miss

- Builders who need browser-session screenshots, deterministic page traces, local credential boundaries, or Playwright-level replay.
- Teams that want model-agnostic, local-first, or code-first agent runtimes outside Microsoft cloud licensing.
- Users who want a single clear product surface instead of Copilot, Studio, Power Automate, Teams, and Azure boundary management.
