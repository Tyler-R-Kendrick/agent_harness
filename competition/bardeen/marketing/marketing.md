# Bardeen Marketing

## Audience

- Sales teams, recruiters, marketers, founders, agencies, freelancers, and productivity users who do repetitive browser work.
- Non-developers who want to scrape, enrich, summarize, draft, and move data between browser apps.
- Teams that are comfortable installing a Chrome extension and connecting SaaS apps.

## Positioning

- Core claim: automate browser apps with AI, ChatGPT, Sheets, and connected web apps.
- Market frame: browser workers waste time switching between apps; Bardeen turns that switching work into shortcuts and playbooks.
- Product promise: use AI prompts, a template catalog, or a no-code builder to create automations without APIs or programming.

## Customer Model

- Freemium acquisition through the Chrome Web Store and playbook catalog.
- Free plan offers core automation, 100 monthly production credits, and unlimited builder-mode testing.
- Paid conversion is driven by credit limits, advanced integrations such as Salesforce/HubSpot, and heavier team workflows.

## Who They Capture

- GTM and recruiting users who need LinkedIn, CRM, spreadsheet, email, and meeting workflows.
- Browser-first operators who value one-click/right-click actions over a separate workflow canvas.
- Users looking for immediate templates instead of developer SDKs.

## Who They Miss

- Developer teams that need local-first agent APIs, protocol-level browser control, or checked-in workflow definitions.
- Security-sensitive teams unwilling to grant broad extension permissions over history, user activity, and active pages.
- Organizations that need auditable run histories, deterministic replay artifacts, or cross-browser runtime guarantees.
