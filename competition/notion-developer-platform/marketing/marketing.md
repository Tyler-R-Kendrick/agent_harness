# Notion Developer Platform Marketing

## Audience

- Teams already running planning, project tracking, and documentation in Notion.
- Operations and RevOps groups that need synced business data and automated handoffs.
- Developers asked to productionize internal AI agents without adding a separate automation stack.

## Positioning

- Core frame: Notion is no longer only a workspace for human collaboration; it is now a runtime for agent collaboration.
- Message spine from launch: "any data, any tool, any agent" inside one workspace.
- The product is presented as an orchestration layer that can coordinate custom logic, external agents, and first-party agents.

## Customer Model

- Broad plan availability with usage credits as the economic governor.
- Workers are temporarily free through August 2026 to reduce adoption friction.
- Expansion path likely grows via deeper enterprise dependency on synced operational data and internal automation.

## Who They Capture

- Existing Notion-heavy companies wanting to centralize workflow automation.
- Teams that prefer workspace-native automation over separate RPA/automation products.
- Operators who value collaborative visibility into agent tasks.

## Who They Miss

- Teams requiring browser-level deterministic automation and replay-grade traceability.
- Buyers prioritizing local-first model execution and strict isolation of runtime surfaces.
- Engineering teams that prefer explicit code-centric automation outside doc/database UIs.
