# Owl Browser Marketing

## Audience

- Data engineering, scraping, QA, security, and AI-agent teams whose Playwright or Puppeteer pipelines are blocked by anti-bot systems.
- Buyers who want self-hosted browser infrastructure rather than a fully managed SaaS browser farm.
- Teams building browser-as-a-service or white-label automation products.

## Positioning

- Core claim: browser automation that does not get blocked.
- Market frame: wrappers around Chromium leak fingerprints, fail CAPTCHAs, and become infrastructure nightmares at 100+ concurrent sessions.
- Product promise: a purpose-built browser engine with stealth, CAPTCHA solving, Tor/proxy control, SDKs, MCP, and Playwright migration support.

## Customer Model

- Entry motion through a low-cost trial and developer plan.
- Higher-value motion through self-hosted, enterprise support, SOC2 controls, SLA, custom integrations, and OEM licensing.

## Who They Capture

- Teams that measure success by reaching difficult public sites.
- Buyers who want to keep browser infrastructure under their own operational control.
- Developers who prefer SDK/API integration over a consumer browser UI.

## Who They Miss

- End users who need a trustworthy daily browser assistant.
- Compliance-heavy teams wary of stealth, Tor, and automatic CAPTCHA messaging.
- Builders who need first-class evidence review more than anti-detection features.
