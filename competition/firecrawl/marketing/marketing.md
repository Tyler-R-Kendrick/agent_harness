# Firecrawl Marketing

## Audience

- AI engineers building agents, RAG systems, competitive intelligence, lead enrichment, price monitoring, and research workflows.
- Coding-agent users who want web search/scrape/MCP tools available inside Claude, Cursor, Windsurf, Codex, or terminal workflows.
- Teams that need web data without maintaining rendering, crawling, proxy, and extraction infrastructure.

## Positioning

- Core claim: the web was built for humans; Firecrawl turns it into machine-usable context for AI systems.
- Product frame: not just scraping, but an infrastructure layer for finding, reading, and acting on live web information.
- Distribution frame: SDKs, REST, CLI, MCP, and agent onboarding skills meet developers where they already build.

## Customer Model

- Open-source adoption builds trust and community gravity.
- Hosted subscription and credit plans monetize reliability, browser sessions, Fire-engine infrastructure, dashboard analytics, and managed scale.
- Enterprise expansion comes from high-volume crawling, scheduled syncs, data pipelines, and reliability around complex pages.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who primarily need clean web content rather than a human-facing browser workspace.
- AI application teams trying to avoid custom scraping pipelines.
- Agent builders who want MCP-accessible web tools with low setup friction.

## Who They Miss

- Users who need local logged-in browsing with visible approvals and a familiar browser surface.
- Teams that require all hosted-browser capabilities in a self-hosted deployment.
- Workflows where the critical artifact is not extracted text, but a replayable human-reviewable browser trace.

## Competitive Read

Firecrawl pressures `agent-browser` by making browser interaction feel like a utility endpoint. `agent-browser` can counter by preserving local authority, visible actions, screenshots, and trace evidence when the user needs to understand what happened, not just receive extracted content.
