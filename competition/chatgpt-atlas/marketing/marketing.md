# ChatGPT Atlas Marketing

## Audience

- Existing ChatGPT users who already ask ChatGPT for search, writing, research, and planning help.
- Knowledge workers, students, shoppers, and business users who want fewer copy/paste loops.
- Enterprises/Edu customers when administrators explicitly enable access.

## Positioning

- Core claim: "The browser with ChatGPT built in."
- Market frame: the browser is where tools, work, and context meet; ChatGPT becomes a super-assistant in that context.
- Product promise: understand what you are looking at, remember useful context, and complete browser tasks.

## Customer Model

- Broad free distribution through a standalone browser.
- Paid value is concentrated in agent mode and business/enterprise controls.
- Atlas strengthens ChatGPT retention by moving ChatGPT from destination app to operating layer.

## Who They Capture

- ChatGPT-loyal users.
- People who trust OpenAI enough to let ChatGPT see pages and act in sessions.
- Business buyers who want vendor-backed controls rather than indie browser tooling.

## Who They Miss

- Users requiring Windows/mobile parity before switching.
- Privacy-first users who dislike memory and cloud processing.
- Developers who need inspectable automation primitives instead of a consumer browser.
