# Kernel Marketing

## Audience

- Developers building web agents, automations, scrapers, and QA flows that need cloud browsers.
- Teams already using Playwright, Puppeteer, CDP, or agent-browser and looking for remote execution.
- Startups that need browser scale, profiles, stealth, and replays without managing infrastructure.

## Positioning

- Core claim: serverless browsers and browser infrastructure for web agents.
- Market frame: browser automation teams should not build lifecycle, scaling, stealth, live view, replay, and auth plumbing themselves.
- Product promise: launch isolated cloud browsers quickly, pay for active use, and inspect or replay sessions when they fail.

## Customer Model

- Free developer usage credits.
- Monthly paid plans add credits and unlock profiles, managed auth, replays, file transfers, extensions, proxies, browser pools, and higher concurrency.
- Enterprise upsell through custom concurrency, retention, compliance, support, and HIPAA BAA.

## Who They Capture

- Engineering teams that already know their browser automation model and need a backend.
- Buyers sensitive to active-time billing and replay retention.
- Existing `agent-browser` users who want a cloud provider switch.

## Who They Miss

- End users looking for a polished AI browser.
- Non-technical teams that want visual workflow authoring or packaged automations.
- Local-only users who do not want authenticated browsing in a managed cloud session.
