# SideButton Marketing

## Audience

- AI coding-agent users working in Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, ChatGPT, VS Code, Windsurf, and other MCP clients.
- Teams migrating legacy internal systems where domain knowledge and repeatable verification matter.
- Builders who want to publish or reuse app-specific agent skills.

## Positioning

- Core claim: one open-source platform for AI agents with browser automation, workflows, and domain knowledge packs.
- Market frame: generic browser tools do not teach an agent how an application works; SideButton packages selectors, workflows, and roles so the agent can operate known apps.
- Product proof: public repo, Apache 2.0 license, quick install command, 40+ browser commands, 60+ REST endpoints, 34+ workflow step types, dashboard, and Chrome extension.

## Customer Model

- Open-source adoption is the primary wedge.
- Knowledge-pack creation and publishing creates marketplace/community gravity.
- Enterprise services are implied by migration and legacy-system verification messaging, but pricing was not visible in the observed public surface.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who want app-specific browser skills instead of one-off Playwright scripts.
- Teams that need repeatable agent workflows for QA, migration, sales, marketing, or operations.
- Organizations willing to encode business process knowledge into structured packs.

## Who They Miss

- Users who want a polished end-user browser assistant rather than infrastructure.
- Buyers who need transparent SaaS pricing before evaluation.
- Security-sensitive teams that are not ready to let a local MCP server, browser extension, and knowledge packs touch authenticated applications.
