# v0 Marketing

## Audience

- Frontend developers, product engineers, designers, founders, and teams already using Vercel or Next.js.
- Builders who want high-quality UI quickly and are comfortable taking generated code into a normal engineering workflow.
- Teams that value Vercel-native deployment, framework defaults, and modern component aesthetics.

## Positioning

- v0 is positioned as a pair programmer that turns natural language into code and UI, with an immediate deployment path to Vercel.
- The brand leans on taste: modern interfaces, React code, design-system awareness, and fast iteration.
- Vercel frames v0 as part of the broader frontend cloud, not as an isolated toy generator.

## Customer Model

- v0 has free and paid plans, with usage now metered by input/output tokens converted to credits.
- Free, Premium, and Team plans include monthly credits; additional work pushes users toward paid usage and the Vercel platform.
- Deployed applications may incur separate Vercel infrastructure charges for compute, bandwidth, storage, domains, and related services.

## Who They Capture

- Teams already standardized on Vercel, Next.js, Tailwind, or shadcn-style UI.
- Developers who want fast scaffolding but still expect to own the code.
- Designers and PMs who need credible UI prototypes that can become code.

## Who They Miss

- Users who want a broad no-code product builder with backend and business logic guided more explicitly.
- Cost-sensitive builders who dislike token/credit ambiguity plus separate hosting usage.
- Teams outside the Vercel/React ecosystem or buyers who need local-first, provider-neutral workflows.
