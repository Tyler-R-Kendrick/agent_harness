# Factory Droids Marketing

## Who They Are Marketing To

- Individual developers who want a stronger coding agent than autocomplete or editor chat.
- Teams that already use VS Code, JetBrains, Vim, terminals, Slack, Jira, GitHub, or CI and do not want to migrate to one cloud IDE.
- Engineering leaders who want agent leverage while keeping approvals, model controls, audit trails, and deployment options.
- Regulated enterprises in finance, government, healthcare, and national-security environments that need ZDR, on-prem, airgapped, or partitioned deployment.

## Positioning

- "Droids: The AI Coding Agent" and "one prompt to PR" position Factory as a full software-work delegation platform.
- "Any interface, any IDE" is the anti-lock-in claim against Cursor, Windsurf, Devin, and model-owner CLIs.
- "Best-in-class model routing" positions Factory as model-agnostic orchestration over Claude, GPT, Gemini, and open-weight pools.
- Enterprise positioning emphasizes governed AI stack integration rather than a consumer coding toy.

## Customer Model

- Pro at $20/month for individuals with desktop, CLI, SDK, cloud and local background agents, billing/usage stats, and agent-readiness dashboard.
- Plus and Max expand rolling usage and add Droid Computers for remote work.
- Teams and Enterprise add seat management, custom limits, onboarding, SSO, SAML/SCIM, ZDR, model controls, audit logging, data residency, network policy, on-prem, and dedicated support.
- Extra Usage is prepaid credit; Droid Core offers a lower-cost fallback model pool after standard usage is exhausted.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who want an agent that follows them across tools instead of living only in one IDE.
- Teams that are ready to standardize agent behavior across issue trackers, terminals, PRs, and CI.
- Enterprise buyers who find Claude Code, Codex CLI, or Cursor too hard to centrally govern.

## Who They Do Not Capture

- Users who primarily want a visible browser workspace with screenshots, tabs, and manual takeover as the central product.
- Budget-sensitive developers who dislike rolling quota windows and model-priced usage.
- Teams that prefer deterministic scripts and browser traces over broad AI-agent delegation.

## Competitive Note For agent-browser

Factory's strongest lesson is that evidence and governance are now product surfaces, not back-office concerns. `agent-browser` should defend with clearer run evidence, browser-level replay, authority boundaries, and deterministic script promotion where Factory currently sells broad Droid orchestration.
