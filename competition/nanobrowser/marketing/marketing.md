# Nanobrowser Marketing

## Audience

- Developers and power users who want browser automation without replacing Chrome.
- Privacy/control-oriented users who prefer open-source code and bring-your-own-key model routing.
- Early adopters comfortable installing browser extensions and configuring providers.

## Positioning

- Core frame: open-source AI web automation in your browser.
- Competes against hosted browser agents by emphasizing local extension control.
- Competes against full AI browsers by avoiding browser migration.

## Customer Model

- Free/open-source distribution.
- Users bear their own model/API costs.
- Commercial upside likely comes from support, hosted services, enterprise packaging, or managed provider setup if the project matures.

## Who They Capture

- Users who want real logged-in browser context without a cloud browser vendor.
- Developers who need hackable agent behavior.
- People who distrust closed AI browsers but still want agentic browsing.

## Who They Miss

- Nontechnical users who do not want API keys or extension permission decisions.
- Enterprises requiring admin-managed compliance, audit, and procurement.
- Teams needing high-reliability headless infrastructure and replay artifacts.
