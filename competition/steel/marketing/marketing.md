# Steel.dev Marketing

## Audience

- AI engineers building agents that need real web interaction.
- Developers moving from local Playwright or Browser Use prototypes into production.
- Teams that want open-source browser infra but may still pay for managed cloud scale.

## Positioning

- Core claim: browser infrastructure for AI agents.
- Market frame: the human web is anti-bot, JavaScript-heavy, authenticated, and expensive for LLMs to consume naively.
- Product promise: control fleets of cloud browsers, reuse cookies, bypass anti-bot friction, reduce tokens, and debug sessions without babysitting browser infrastructure.

## Customer Model

- Open-source project for trust, community, and local/self-host adoption.
- Hosted plans convert users through browser-hour credits, proxy bandwidth, CAPTCHA solves, and convenience.
- Developer community channels such as GitHub, Discord, cookbook examples, and integrations create bottom-up distribution.

## Who They Capture

- Builders who want infrastructure primitives rather than a finished end-user browser.
- Teams that need Docker/self-host as a procurement or debugging fallback.
- Agent-framework users who need a CDP-compatible cloud browser.

## Who They Miss

- Non-technical end users and teams expecting no-code workflow building.
- Buyers who need enterprise governance and polished admin surfaces before adopting.
- Users who want fully local personal browsing instead of remote browser sessions.
