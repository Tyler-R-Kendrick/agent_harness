# webact Marketing

## Audience

- Developers using Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, Windsurf, Cline, Copilot, OpenCode, Goose, or other MCP-capable agents.
- Agent builders frustrated by Playwright install size, bundled Chromium sessions, and verbose accessibility snapshots.
- Local-first users who want their actual Chrome user agent, cookies, and logins.

## Positioning

- Core claim: browser control for AI agents with minimal tokens.
- Competitive frame: Playwright-based tools add abstraction layers, large installs, detached browser sessions, and verbose snapshots.
- Product proof: single Rust binary, zero dependencies, 60+ CDP commands, compact page briefs, and direct comparisons against agent-browser/Playwright MCP/Stagehand/Browser Use output size.

## Customer Model

- Open-source MIT distribution.
- No visible hosted pricing or enterprise plan at snapshot time.
- Adoption likely comes through tool virality, HN/GitHub discussion, and skill/MCP compatibility.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who want a small, fast browser bridge and dislike framework-heavy automation.
- Cost-sensitive agent users who see browser snapshots dominate token spend.
- Users who want real Chrome sessions without installing a separate browser runtime.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprise teams needing centralized audit, policy, and support.
- Users who want a graphical operator console, approval queue, or durable evidence store.
- Teams already standardized on Playwright for test compatibility and trace tooling.
