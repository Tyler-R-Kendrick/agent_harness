# Windsurf Cascade Marketing

## Who They Are Marketing To

- Developers who want an AI-native editor with less setup than a terminal-only agent.
- VS Code and Cursor users who can migrate extensions and settings into a familiar code-editor shell.
- Web-app builders who value integrated previews, deploys, and fast agent loops.
- Teams that want centralized billing, admin dashboards, analytics, automated ZDR, SSO, RBAC, priority support, and hybrid deployment.

## Positioning

- Windsurf positions itself as "the best AI for coding" and an editor where developers are doing their best work.
- Cascade is positioned as the collaborative agent with deep codebase understanding, real-time awareness, advanced tools, and an ability to think several steps ahead.
- Since Cognition, the homepage positions Windsurf as local Cascade plus Devin cloud agents: plan locally, hand off complex work, and keep coding.
- Enterprise positioning leans on security, trust, team controls, and testimonials rather than only individual productivity.

## Customer Model

- Plans include Free, Pro, Max, Teams, and Enterprise with model usage limits and paid add-on usage.
- Teams is marketed at $40/user/month on the enterprise page, with centralized billing, admin dashboard, analytics, priority support, and automated ZDR.
- Enterprise is sales-led and adds dedicated account management, RBAC, SSO/access controls, and hybrid deployment.
- Usage-based changes in 2026 moved the product away from simple prompt-credit expectations and created user sensitivity around metering.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who want a polished IDE-native agent rather than terminal delegation.
- Teams that already accept cloud AI coding tools but need centralized account controls.
- Web-app creators who want to move from edit to preview/deploy inside one product.
- Users who like continuous AI collaboration and memories/rules rather than one-shot task agents.

## Who They Do Not Capture

- Developers who prefer local-first CLI agents with explicit shell output and Git ownership.
- Users who dislike model-priced or quota-priced agent work.
- Buyers whose core problem is browser-session evidence, authenticated web control, screenshots, or cross-tab audit rather than code editing.

## Competitive Note For agent-browser

Windsurf proves that agent work benefits from being embedded in the daily work surface. `agent-browser` should match that ergonomic lesson for browser work: keep manual browsing, agent actions, screenshots, logs, approvals, and recovery in one visible workspace.
