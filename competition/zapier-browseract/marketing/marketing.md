# Zapier BrowserAct Marketing

## Audience

- Existing Zapier users who want AI assistants to trigger browser workflows.
- Developers who want MCP access to automation without building and hosting a server.
- Operations teams that already split work between Zapier, agents, and browser-only tasks.

## Positioning

- Core claim: use BrowserAct MCP to take action in AI tools.
- Product frame: no-code in Zapier Agents, or flexible developer use through any MCP-compatible environment.
- Ecosystem claim: Zapier MCP provides authenticated access to thousands of integrations and tens of thousands of searches/actions.

## Customer Model

- Available to Zapier accounts.
- One MCP tool call consumes two Zapier tasks.
- Upsell is tied to task quota, Zapier plan expansion, and higher-volume automation use.

## Who They Capture

- Teams already using Zapier as the automation backbone.
- Users who want a quick MCP endpoint rather than local browser tooling.
- Operators who view browser automation as a supplement to app-to-app workflows.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprise accounts that need app/action restrictions honored through MCP today.
- Browser-first developers who need screenshots, traces, HAR/console evidence, or deterministic replay.
- Local-first users who do not want browser work mediated through a cloud workflow platform.
