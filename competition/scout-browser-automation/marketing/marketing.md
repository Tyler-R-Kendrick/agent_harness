# Scout Marketing

## Audience

- Developers using Claude, Gemini, GPT, Cursor, VS Code, Cline, or custom MCP agents.
- Teams that want browser automation against authenticated pages without remote browser infrastructure.
- Builders who need extraction, testing, debugging, research, mobile viewport checks, and network observation from the same browser tool surface.

## Positioning

- Core claim: control any browser from any AI agent.
- Market frame: remote browser farms, proxy layers, MITM setups, selector scraping, and proprietary APIs add friction and fragility.
- Product promise: extension-first MCP plus CDP, real sessions, accessibility snapshots, DOM extraction, network and console events, multi-agent coordination, and guardrails.

## Customer Model

- Free plan includes trial credits.
- Pro at $9.99/month unlocks browser tooling and the MCP surface.
- AI inference is separate: BYOK is free, or hosted inference starts as an addon.
- Additional voice, system, and bundle tiers expand beyond browser automation.

## Who They Capture

- Protocol-minded developers who want precise tools and explicit docs.
- Users who want authenticated Chrome access without shipping credentials to a remote browser vendor.
- Teams with token-cost sensitivity because snapshot filtering and result truncation are first-class.

## Who They Miss

- Users who prefer a fully local no-account tool.
- Buyers who do not want wallet/payment tools near browser automation settings.
- Non-technical users who expect a simple conversational browser assistant.
