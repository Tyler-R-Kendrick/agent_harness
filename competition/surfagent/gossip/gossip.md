# SurfAgent Gossip

## Public sentiment

- ClawHub lists SurfAgent as a real-browser skill and reinforces the pitch around persistent cookies, sessions, screenshots, crawl, map, and MCP/HTTP access.
- ClawHub also flags an important security concern: a local daemon and `npx`-installed MCP tooling can access sensitive browser session data if the endpoint or tool chain is abused.
- Broader browser-agent chatter supports SurfAgent's wedge: users value local logged-in Chrome sessions because they avoid login overhead and bot-detection problems.

## Design and UX complaints to watch

- The product's power comes from a sensitive asset: a real logged-in local browser. Consent, scopes, endpoint binding, and data egress need to be visible, not buried.
- One-time pricing is attractive, but buyers may wonder about update cadence, support guarantees, and multi-device management.
- Bot-detection and scraping language can attract power users while making compliance-sensitive teams cautious.
