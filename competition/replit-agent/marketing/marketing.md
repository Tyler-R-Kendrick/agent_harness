# Replit Agent Marketing

## Audience

- Non-coders and semi-technical builders who want to ship apps from plain language.
- Startup operators, students, educators, and solo builders who value browser-based setup and deployment.
- Teams that want chat, code, preview, database/auth, design, and publishing in one tool.

## Positioning

- Agent is framed as a creative partner that takes action, not a chatbot.
- The strongest message is "describe what you want and publish it" across web apps, mobile apps, dashboards, slides, files, and designs.
- Replit sells an integrated workspace: Agent, Canvas, task system, connectors, Auth, database, deployments, and collaboration.

## Customer Model

- Starter has capped Agent usage and one published app.
- Core and Pro unlock full builds, Plan Mode, connectors, task planning, published apps, and background task concurrency.
- Agent work is billed through effort-based pricing and credits, with third-party API costs deducted from the user's Replit balance.

## Who They Capture

- Users who do not want local setup.
- Builders who prioritize visual preview and deployment over codebase control.
- Teams that want quick prototypes with hosted infrastructure bundled in.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who want local-first state, repo-native tests, and explicit PR review gates.
- Buyers who distrust variable credit burn.
- Teams that need auditable browser-agent traces rather than app-builder checkpoints.
