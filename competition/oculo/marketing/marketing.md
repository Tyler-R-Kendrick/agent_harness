# Oculo Marketing

## Audience

- Developers using Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, VS Code, or other MCP-aware clients.
- Users who want a real browser rather than Playwright, Puppeteer, or a detached headless session.
- Privacy-focused users who want local browsing, no telemetry, PII redaction, and OS keychain credential storage.
- Browser-agent builders concerned about token costs from full DOM snapshots.

## Positioning

- Core claim: an AI browser that gives agents eyes and hands for the web.
- Competitive frame: traditional browser automation wastes tokens and runs outside the user's daily browser; Oculo is a real browser with MCP built in.
- Design analogy: "Cursor is to VS Code what Oculo is to Chrome" makes the product category legible to developer buyers.

## Customer Model

- Free, open-source, MIT-licensed app.
- No visible subscriptions, paid tiers, usage limits, or telemetry at snapshot time.
- Distribution appears to be developer-led through GitHub, MCP server directories, and direct desktop downloads.

## Who They Capture

- Users who want local MCP browser control with a polished app wrapper.
- Developers who want a concise tool surface instead of hundreds of raw browser endpoints.
- Cost-sensitive agent users who notice browser context dominating model input.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprises needing cloud-managed browser fleets, centralized policy, and audit retention.
- Users who do not want to switch primary browsers.
- Teams that already standardized on Playwright trace tooling and browser infrastructure.
