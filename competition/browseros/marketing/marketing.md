# BrowserOS Marketing

## Audience

- Developers and power users who distrust closed AI browsers.
- Founders, researchers, sales, marketers, designers, and executive assistants with cross-app repetitive workflows.
- Users of Codex, Claude Code, Gemini CLI, and MCP ecosystems.

## Positioning

- Core claim: "The Open Source Agentic Browser."
- Market frame: browsers should be open-source and privacy-first, not tracking surfaces for search/ad companies.
- Product promise: automate tasks locally, bring your own model, connect apps via MCP, and run on macOS/Windows/Linux.

## Customer Model

- Free and open source.
- Monetization likely adjacent to support, cloud services, enterprise, or managed integrations, but the public pitch centers on no browser subscription.
- Users bring local models or pay model providers directly.

## Who They Capture

- Privacy-first AI browser users.
- Developers who want a browser automation surface that works with coding agents.
- Users who want Chrome extension compatibility without Chrome/Google positioning.

## Who They Miss

- Nontechnical users who prefer OpenAI/Perplexity defaults and account integration.
- Enterprises needing polished admin, compliance, and procurement workflows today.
- Users who equate open-source with more maintenance burden.
