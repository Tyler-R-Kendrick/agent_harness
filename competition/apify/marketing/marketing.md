# Apify Marketing

## Audience

- Growth, data, sales, SEO, ecommerce, research, and engineering teams that need fresh web data.
- AI-agent builders who need tools that return structured external data rather than raw browsing transcripts.
- Developers and agencies that want to publish and monetize reusable web automation.

## Positioning

- Core claim: connect AI agents to fresh web data with thousands of ready-made scrapers and automations.
- Market frame: websites are hard to scrape, agent tools need structured outputs, and teams should reuse marketplace Actors before building custom browser automations.
- Agent positioning: native integrations with LangGraph, CrewAI, Mastra, OpenAPI, and MCP make Apify a tool supply layer for agents.

## Customer Model

- Free tier gives monthly credits.
- Paid plans bundle prepaid usage and lower compute-unit pricing at higher tiers.
- Additional spend can come from Store Actor pricing, compute units, residential/datacenter proxies, storage, data transfer, support, and enterprise custom plans.
- Creator-side monetization pulls developers into the ecosystem and expands catalog supply.

## Who They Capture

- Buyers who care more about output data than controlling a browser.
- Teams that want a proven scraper for a named site this week.
- Agent platforms that need lots of callable tools exposed through MCP or APIs.

## Who They Miss

- Teams that need local-first browser sessions with user-visible approvals and trace governance.
- Users who want one coherent browser workspace rather than a marketplace of jobs.
- Buyers who require every automation to be maintained in-house or self-hosted end to end.
