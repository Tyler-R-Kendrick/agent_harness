# UiPath Agentic Automation Gossip

## Positive Signals

- Gartner Peer Insights presents UiPath as an agentic business orchestration platform with a unified low-code/pro-code builder, document understanding, testing, and cloud/on-prem deployment flexibility.
- Reddit practitioners still describe UiPath as serious automation infrastructure, especially for stable high-volume workflows.
- The "agents think, robots do, people lead" split resonates with practitioners who believe agents should handle exceptions while deterministic automation owns the happy path.

## Negative Signals

- Reddit RPA practitioners worry that stakeholders are buying "agents for the sake of agents" and accepting lower automation coverage because agentic is a budget keyword.
- Multiple community threads mention UiPath cost at scale as a major objection.
- Day-to-day users report bugs and module unevenness, even when they also acknowledge support and breadth.

## Bugs And UX Friction To Watch

- Platform sprawl: Autopilot, Studio, Maestro, robots, apps, test, process mining, and document AI all create onboarding surface area.
- Agentic runtime uncertainty can make ROI harder to defend than deterministic bot programs.
- Browser/UI automation remains vulnerable when target apps change; the Healing Agent message is valuable precisely because this pain is persistent.
