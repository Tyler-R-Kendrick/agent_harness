# Solace AGI Gossip

## Positive Signals

- Category sentiment favors tools that make browser-agent execution, state, and recovery explicit. Solace's approval and evidence language is aligned with that demand.
- Local-first and BYOK positioning can reduce anxiety around screenshots, cookies, and logged-in account data.
- Recipe replay speaks to a common complaint that LLM browser automation is too expensive and repetitive when tasks recur.

## Negative Signals

- Public third-party discussion is still thin compared with better-known tools, so the strongest signals come from product copy rather than broad user proof.
- Broad claims around Software 5.0, OAuth3, evidence vaults, and regulated audit trails may read as premature until customers can inspect concrete artifacts.
- In the wider browser-automation category, users complain that agents still fail around state, recovery, and brittle execution; Solace's UX must prove those problems are solved, not only governed.

## Bug And UX Risk Themes

- Approval prompts can become fatiguing if the product cannot group low-risk actions or show clear risk deltas.
- Recipe replay can fail when site layouts or permissions drift unless evidence captures the exact divergence.
- Pricing/localization inconsistencies can create uncertainty about plan limits and overages.
