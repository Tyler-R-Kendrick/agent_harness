# Solace AGI Marketing

## Target Customer

- Operators and small teams with repetitive browser work in Gmail, Slack, LinkedIn, exports, and SaaS dashboards.
- Developers and AI-coding-assistant users who want a browser automation surface that can be discovered by Claude, GPT-4, or any LLM via API/MCP-style integration.
- Regulated or audit-sensitive teams that care about evidence retention, approval trails, role-based permissions, and budget limits.

## Positioning

- "Local-first" is the headline trust wedge: work runs on the user's machine, with screenshots and cloud evidence opt-in.
- The product contrasts itself with blind background automation by showing actions before they execute.
- It sells recipes as durable automation assets: create once, replay cheaply, preserve evidence.

## Customer Model

- Free local/BYOK tier for adoption and trust.
- Paid tiers add managed LLMs, team permissions, evidence retention, cloud twin, and enterprise onboarding.
- Marketing compares paid plans to virtual-assistant labor, signaling a productivity buyer more than a browser-infrastructure buyer.

## Who They Capture

- Buyers anxious about opaque agents clicking through sensitive accounts.
- Teams that need a human-in-the-loop browser agent with audit artifacts.
- Users who want reusable automations without building a Playwright or browser-agent stack.

## Who They Do Not Capture

- Developers who want a compact, code-native browser state API rather than a new browser/productivity platform.
- Teams that already prefer cloud browser farms, parallel headless sessions, or pure SDK control.
- Buyers skeptical of broad governance terminology before proven reliability metrics.
