# Microsoft Edge Copilot Mode Marketing

## Audience

- Mainstream Edge users on Windows and macOS.
- Microsoft account users already exposed to Copilot across Windows, Microsoft 365, Bing, and Edge.
- Shoppers, researchers, students, and knowledge workers who need summaries, comparisons, voice commands, and routine web-task help.

## Positioning

- Core claim: "Your AI browser."
- Market frame: traditional tab-based browsing is too linear and manual; Copilot should help users browse, compare, resume, and act.
- Product promise: smart routing between search/chat/navigation, hands-free voice browsing, cross-tab/history summaries, Actions, Journeys, and shopping help.

## Customer Model

- Bundled into Microsoft Edge with preview/availability caveats.
- Some coverage notes a limited free period and future availability changes, so long-term monetization remains unclear.
- Microsoft can subsidize adoption through Windows, Bing, Microsoft account, and Copilot ecosystem strategy.

## Who They Capture

- Users already inside the Microsoft ecosystem.
- People who want AI features without installing a new browser.
- Users who trust Microsoft permission settings enough to allow tab/history context.

## Who They Miss

- Users frustrated by Copilot promotion, AI-first new tabs, or browser bloat.
- Privacy-first users who do not want history/context feeding a cloud AI assistant.
- Developers who need transparent automation traces, local-first execution, or programmable browser-agent APIs.
