# AgentSmith Gossip

## Positive Signals

- The landing page is clear about use cases and pricing, which many agent products avoid.
- The product is tuned for practical jobs like lead research, price monitoring, job applications, and data extraction.
- The local extension story responds to user anxiety about hosted automation touching browsing data.

## Negative Signals

- Public proof is incomplete where the page says screenshots or demo video are coming soon.
- Community footprint appears early, so reliability claims should be tested before operational adoption.
- The name overlaps unrelated "AgentSmith" security stories in search results, which can muddy discovery and trust.

## Category Chatter

- Browser-agent users repeatedly say reliability matters more than planning: agents fail when pages shift, modals appear, and flows change.
- Consumer users like simple extension packaging, but browser agents that can act inside logged-in sites raise privacy and wrong-click concerns.
- Action-based pricing is easier than token pricing, but users still worry when an autonomous loop burns through metered steps.

## Bug And UX Risks To Watch

- Replayed workflows may become brittle when target sites redesign forms or selectors.
- Local scratchpad data for resumes or personal information needs clear boundaries and deletion controls.
- "No account" onboarding is strong, but support, sync, and team controls may require account concepts later.
- Users may confuse observed step-by-step action feedback with full auditability unless logs are durable and exportable.
