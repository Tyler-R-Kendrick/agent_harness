# Chromeflow Marketing

## Target Customer

- Claude Code and Codex users who already ask agents to build software but still manually operate web dashboards.
- Developers setting up Stripe, GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, Render, LinkedIn, YouTube, or other browser-first workflows.
- Local-session users who prefer visible real Chrome over a fresh Playwright profile or remote VM.

## Positioning

- Chromeflow positions itself as the missing browser hand for coding agents.
- It competes against manual tab-switching more than against generic browser hosting.
- The strongest claim is "real Chrome with your sessions" plus pauses for the moments a human must own.

## Customer Model

- Open-source/free distribution through plugin and extension installation.
- The likely acquisition motion is developer community proof, Reddit demos, and task-specific examples rather than enterprise procurement.
- It captures attention by naming Claude Code and Codex directly.

## Who They Capture

- Power users who want agents to operate SaaS dashboards that lack MCP integrations.
- Developers who value local login reuse and visible browser state.
- Users frustrated by cloud browser profiles, service-specific MCP setup, or screenshot-only computer-use loops.

## Who They Do Not Capture

- Teams that need managed parallel browser infrastructure, audit retention, access controls, or enterprise support.
- Buyers who cannot approve an extension/native-host bridge into their primary browser profile.
- Users who want deterministic test artifacts rather than guided one-off setup work.
