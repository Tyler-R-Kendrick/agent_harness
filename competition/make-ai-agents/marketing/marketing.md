# Make AI Agents Marketing

## Audience

- Business operations, finance, sales, support, and automation teams already using Make.
- No-code builders who want AI decisions inside durable app workflows.
- Mid-market and enterprise buyers who need visibility before scaling AI automation.

## Positioning

- Core frame: build AI agents you can trust.
- Make positions agents as transparent, controllable, reusable parts of business automation rather than free-floating chatbots.
- The pitch emphasizes thousands of app connections, visible decisions, scalable control, and team sharing.

## Customer Model

- Plan-based Make adoption with AI Agents available broadly and deeper provider choices on paid plans.
- Teams pay through Make plan economics, operations, and any model/provider usage implied by agent runs.
- Case studies position agents as cost savers for invoice, expense, support, and workflow-heavy processes.

## Who They Capture

- Existing Make users who want AI without leaving the canvas.
- Teams that need visual approval from non-engineering stakeholders.
- Businesses automating repeatable internal processes across many SaaS apps.

## Who They Miss

- Developers who need a browser execution environment, replay traces, or Playwright-level escape hatches.
- Users who want local-first control over logged-in browser profiles.
- Buyers who prefer code-first agent tests and checked-in eval fixtures over visual scenarios.
