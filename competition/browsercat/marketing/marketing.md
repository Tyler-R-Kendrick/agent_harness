# BrowserCat Marketing

## Audience

- Developers who already use Playwright, Puppeteer, or CDP and do not want to manage browser infrastructure.
- Small teams running UI tests, web scraping, PDF/image/video generation, or web-monitoring jobs.
- AI-agent builders who need cloud browsers but do not want a full workflow platform.
- Buyers who care about predictable usage controls and low-cost experimentation.

## Positioning

- Core claim: browser automation without the pain and expense of hosting headless browsers.
- Competitive frame: open-protocol browser capacity with no vendor lock-in and a simple one-line migration from local Playwright.
- The site also markets BrowserCat Migrate, which uses browser automation and AI-editable code as a nearby website-modernization offer.

## Customer Model

- Free Hobby plan with included monthly credits.
- Business plan starts at $50/month with included credits and lower overage pricing.
- Enterprise is contact-sales, still framed around the same protocol support and global browser fleet.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who want a faster path than standing up EC2, Docker, browser patching, and Playwright version management.
- Cost-sensitive builders who need remote browsers but are not ready for enterprise scraping platforms.
- Teams that want utility APIs and direct browser sessions under one usage model.

## Who They Miss

- Local-first users who need to preserve authenticated browser state on their own machine.
- Compliance-heavy buyers that need detailed audit logs, retention controls, and policy surfaces before remote browsing.
- Users who need an agent-facing UI with approvals, step history, screenshots, and recovery controls rather than a raw automation endpoint.
