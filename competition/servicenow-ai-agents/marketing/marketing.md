# ServiceNow AI Agents Marketing

## Audience

- CIOs, service-management leaders, HR operations, security/risk teams, customer service leaders, and platform owners already invested in ServiceNow.
- Enterprises worried about AI sprawl, unmanaged agents, shadow automation, and lack of measurable AI ROI.
- Developers building ServiceNow apps who want natural-language Build Agent help inside ServiceNow Studio or external coding tools.

## Positioning

- ServiceNow positions itself as the "AI control tower" for business reinvention and governed autonomous work.
- The company contrasts old advisory copilots with agents that can sense, decide, and act under organizational guardrails.
- Marketing emphasizes autonomous workforce expansion across IT, CRM, HR, security, risk, and customer service, with Control Tower governing any agent or workflow.

## Customer Model

- Sales-led expansion into existing ServiceNow accounts, especially Pro Plus, Now Assist, App Engine, AI Agent Studio, and AI Control Tower buyers.
- Packaged specialists and platform-native workflows help ServiceNow sell business outcomes rather than generic agent tooling.
- Governance is a land-and-expand motion: discover AI activity, measure value, manage risk, then route more agent work through the ServiceNow platform.

## Who They Capture

- Large enterprises with mature ServiceNow estates and compliance-heavy workflows.
- Leaders who prioritize approvals, kill switches, ROI reporting, and data/control ownership over a lightweight builder.
- ServiceNow developers and admins who want AI assistance without leaving the platform governance model.

## Who They Miss

- Teams that need local browser operation, visible page-level evidence, screenshots, and deterministic replay outside ServiceNow records.
- Smaller organizations without clean ServiceNow data or the budget for broad platform AI expansion.
- Builders who prefer open, code-first, or model-agnostic agent runtimes over a ServiceNow-centered control plane.
