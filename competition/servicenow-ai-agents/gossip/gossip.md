# ServiceNow AI Agents Gossip

## Positive Signals

- Knowledge 2026 attendees recognized AI Control Tower as addressing a real executive problem: most enterprises do not know which agents are running, what they cost, or who can pause them.
- ServiceNow practitioners see value when AI respects domain separation, approvals, and platform permissions.
- Build Agent's extension into major coding tools makes ServiceNow feel less isolated from the modern developer-agent workflow.

## Negative Signals

- Community skepticism focuses on keynote hype versus the difficulty of making autonomous agents work against messy enterprise data.
- Users worry that agents need context scattered across CMDB records, email threads, Slack approvals, and outdated docs, which a single platform may not fully capture.
- Early AI Agent experiences are described as hit-or-miss, with account teams sometimes struggling to provide deeper implementation expertise.

## Bug And Friction Themes

- AI billing and usage visibility are recurring concerns, especially across production and subproduction instances.
- The governance story can feel like overhead if customers have not yet proven agent value.
- Prompt-injection and over-permissioned ServiceNow APIs are high-stakes risks because agents may touch authoritative enterprise records.
