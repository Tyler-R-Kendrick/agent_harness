# Perplexity Comet Design

## Look And Feel

- Consumer browser landing page uses bold product copy, animated/screenshot-heavy feature panels, and repeated assistant task examples.
- Product language is short and action-oriented: "AI that understands", "AI that builds", "AI that emails", "AI that shops".
- The browser itself is marketed as familiar enough to switch to, with the AI assistant as the primary differentiator.

## Design Tokens To Track

```yaml
surface: dark/space-inspired marketing with high-contrast browser screenshots
accent: Perplexity blue/teal and cosmic branding cues
primary_control: assistant/chat affordance inside browser
task_cards:
  - understand
  - build
  - email
  - create
  - shop
trust_controls:
  - pause assistant
  - privacy settings FAQ
information_density: marketing low; assistant task flow medium
```

## Differentiators

- Comet connects Perplexity's answer engine directly to browsing and task delegation.
- Strong consumer task framing: email, shopping, study planning, finance, vacations.
- Broad platform availability is part of the design signal: Mac, Windows, iOS, Android.

## What Is Good

- Clear benefit framing: each feature is a concrete delegated job rather than an abstract AI capability.
- Search-native heritage makes research and summarization easy to understand.
- The design gives users simple mental models for what the assistant can do.

## Where It Breaks Down

- "Do anything" framing overpromises against current reliability and safety limits.
- Consumer screenshots and marketing underplay the agent audit trail problem.
- The assistant's access to logged-in web contexts makes privacy/security controls central, but they are less visually prominent than capability claims.
