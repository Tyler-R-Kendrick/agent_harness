# Perplexity Comet Marketing

## Audience

- Perplexity users who already prefer answer-engine search over Google.
- Productivity users who want AI to summarize, compare, and delegate routine tasks.
- Mobile users as Comet expands across iOS and Android.

## Positioning

- Core claim: "The browser that works for you."
- Market frame: browsing should be assistant-led and less friction-heavy.
- Product promise: delegate inbox, groceries, research, finance, and travel workflows.

## Customer Model

- Initial scarcity/premium positioning around high-tier Perplexity subscriptions.
- Broader free access grows browser share and creates Pro/Max upsell.
- Search distribution and ad/user-profile strategy are strategically important.

## Who They Capture

- Research-heavy users who like Perplexity answers.
- Users willing to try an AI-native browser without waiting for Google/Apple.
- Consumers motivated by assistant-like chores rather than developer automation.

## Who They Miss

- Security-sensitive users after public prompt-injection reports.
- Users who need deterministic automation or auditable action traces.
- Users skeptical of ad-targeting or browsing-profile incentives.
