# Selenium WebDriver And Grid Marketing

## Audience

- QA organizations with established automated testing programs.
- Enterprises that need standards-based cross-browser and cross-platform coverage.
- Developers and SDETs who prefer language bindings and CI integration over hosted agent workflows.

## Positioning

Selenium positions itself as the browser automation project: a community-maintained umbrella for tools and libraries that support web browser automation.

Against `agent-browser`, Selenium's strongest positioning is trust through age, standards, and breadth. It does not need to claim agentic intelligence because it already owns the baseline automation vocabulary in many organizations.

## Customer Model

- Free open-source adoption.
- Ecosystem sponsors and commercial partners around cloud browser infrastructure, visual testing, and enterprise QA services.
- Procurement influence through tools that embed Selenium-compatible WebDriver or Grid infrastructure.

## Who It Captures

- QA teams that already have CI, test suites, reporting, and browser matrix requirements.
- Enterprises that care more about standards and repeatable tests than natural-language delegation.
- Developers who want language-native control and are willing to manage test architecture.

## Who It Does Not Capture

- Users who want a visible AI workbench with approvals and explanations.
- Product teams that need screenshots, recordings, and action traces organized as agent evidence rather than test output.
- Operators who want browser work without writing or maintaining code.

## Competitive Takeaway

Selenium sets the bar for browser breadth and standards. `agent-browser` can differentiate by pairing agent-native UX and evidence with enough interoperability that Selenium-shaped buyers do not see it as a toy.
