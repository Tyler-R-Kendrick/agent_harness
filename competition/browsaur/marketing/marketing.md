# Browsaur Marketing

## Audience

- AI agent teams that need web automation to pass bot checks.
- Scraping, lead-generation, ecommerce, and data-pipeline developers.
- Teams already using Steel, Browserbase, Browserless, Bright Data, Playwright, Puppeteer, or CDP.
- Cost-sensitive builders with access to Mac mini hardware.

## Positioning

- Core claim: real Chrome browsers for AI agents that need to get through.
- Market frame: Linux Chromium in datacenters is detectable and expensive once proxy costs are counted.
- Product promise: real macOS Chrome, residential IPs, profile persistence, first-party MCP, open-source CLI, and free self-hosting.

## Customer Model

- Self-hosted plan is free forever under an MIT-licensed model.
- Cloud starts at $10/month for 50 browser hours, with a $49/month team plan for larger workloads.
- Volume pricing is available for heavier use.
- The commercial wedge is operational convenience and residential Mac hardware rather than proprietary SDK lock-in.

## Who They Capture

- Developers who have been blocked by Cloudflare, Datadome, or headless-browser fingerprinting.
- Teams that want CDP/Playwright compatibility and clear pricing.
- Buyers who prefer a credible self-host escape hatch.

## Who They Miss

- Users who need a human-facing browser-agent workbench.
- Enterprise teams that need policy, approval queues, audit review, and role-based governance more than stealth.
- Customers uncomfortable with residential-IP automation positioning.
