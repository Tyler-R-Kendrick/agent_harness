# Warpsurf Marketing

## Audience

- Developers, power users, and AI-agent experimenters who want a browser copilot in Chrome without a hosted browser platform.
- Privacy-sensitive users who prefer local execution and bring-your-own model keys.
- Open-source contributors interested in browser automation, multi-agent workflows, and local AI tooling.

## Positioning

- Core claim: an open-source AI browser copilot for Chrome that can chat, search, or deploy autonomous agents.
- Trust claim: runs locally, keeps browsing data on the user's machine, and uses URL firewall controls.
- UX claim: agents should be fast, visible, interruptible, and cost-aware.

## Customer Model

- Chrome Web Store and source-code distribution drive adoption.
- BYO keys shift model billing to users and avoid a platform subscription story.
- Community growth comes from demos, GitHub contribution, and practical browser-agent workflows.

## Who They Capture

- Users who want real logged-in browser context without remote browser farms.
- Builders who want model choice and local extension control.
- Early adopters who value transparency, trajectory views, and multi-agent experiments over managed support.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprises needing centralized policy, compliance, admin, and support.
- Users who do not want to configure model keys or understand model costs.
- Teams that need stable replay, regression suites, or production run artifacts rather than a research copilot.

## Competitive Read

Warpsurf is a direct local-browser wedge against `agent-browser`: side panel, real tabs, BYO keys, URL firewall, task estimates, and visible trajectories. `agent-browser` needs to stay ahead on durable evidence, testable reliability, approval semantics, and workspace integration.
