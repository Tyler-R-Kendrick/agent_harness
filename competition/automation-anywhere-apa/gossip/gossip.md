# Automation Anywhere APA Gossip

## Positive Signals

- G2 lists thousands of reviews for Automation Anywhere and now frames the product under Agentic Process Automation.
- Community and review surfaces credit Automation Anywhere for user-friendly automation and a mature enterprise RPA footprint.
- The EnterpriseClaw listing makes the AI-agent story tangible by exposing documents, browser use, code writing, memory, and tool toggles as package capabilities.

## Negative Signals

- RPA practitioners on Reddit say Automation Anywhere is pushing agents hard, sometimes into use cases where classic RPA is cheaper and more predictable.
- Pricing for full agentic features is repeatedly called out as high, with currency exchange making it worse for some non-US teams.
- Users worry that agentic marketing may outrun mature production behavior.

## Bugs And UX Friction To Watch

- Agentic package setup can still require dependencies, workstation validation, model configuration, and governance review.
- A marketplace package hides implementation complexity until a team must debug failures in local apps, websites, or credentials.
- Goal-prompt autonomy makes audit and replay more important than traditional fixed bot logs.
