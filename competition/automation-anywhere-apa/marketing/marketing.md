# Automation Anywhere APA Marketing

## Audience

- Enterprise automation leaders, shared-service organizations, back-office operations, and RPA centers of excellence.
- Buyers that want a cloud-native control room and packaged automation marketplace.
- Teams modernizing from attended/unattended bots toward AI-agent-assisted process execution.

## Positioning

- Automation Anywhere says APA combines AI agents, RPA, APIs, document processing, orchestration, and governance.
- The company contrasts itself with Salesforce Agentforce or Microsoft Copilot by positioning APA as a layer above both SaaS and legacy applications.
- EnterpriseClaw and other app-store assets show a strategy of packaged agents for business tasks rather than only developer primitives.

## Customer Model

- Sales-led enterprise platform.
- Marketplace/app-store packaging creates a procurement and reuse channel for automations.
- Land-and-expand path through existing RPA programs, then upsell AI agents and process orchestration.

## Captures

- Enterprises that already run Automation Anywhere bots.
- Business teams that prefer package deployment and platform governance over custom code.
- Buyers that want cloud-native automation rather than self-hosted or local-first tooling.

## Misses

- Developers who want transparent browser traces, Playwright-level control, or local agent state.
- Teams sensitive to agentic feature pricing.
- Use cases where a deterministic bot or API integration is easier to validate than autonomous goal execution.
