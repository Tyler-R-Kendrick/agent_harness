# Automation Anywhere APA Design

## Look And Feel

- Enterprise automation marketing with bold agentic language, high usage metrics, and business-process outcomes.
- Bot Store surfaces look like app listings: benefits, tasks, prerequisites, package compatibility, and implementation notes.
- Product pages lean toward "system above systems" orchestration rather than a visible browser session UI.

## Differentiators

- Automation Anywhere claims an Agentic Process Automation category rather than generic RPA.
- EnterpriseClaw preview makes the product category concrete: an autonomous agent can manipulate documents, browse the web, write code, and complete multi-step tasks from a goal prompt.
- Bot Store/App Store packaging gives non-core teams a marketplace path to deploy specialized automations.

## What Is Good

- Strong "build once, run anywhere" language for Windows/macOS workstations.
- Marketplace packaging makes demos and adoption easier than starting with blank orchestration primitives.
- Cloud-native Control Room appeals to companies that do not want to manage automation infrastructure.

## Where It Breaks Down

- Product and community language can over-index on agentic novelty, even when classic RPA is more predictable.
- Preview agent packages create uncertainty around production readiness and support expectations.
- Pricing and full agentic feature access can be a barrier for smaller teams and non-US buyers.
