# Opera Neon Marketing

## Audience

- AI power users who already spend significant time with chatbots, research workflows, and coding/generation tools.
- Opera loyalists and browser enthusiasts willing to try experimental browser experiences.
- Users who want native browser automation without giving passwords to a separate cloud-browser service.

## Positioning

- Core claim: Opera Neon is an agentic browser for the AI web.
- Market frame: browsers are moving from passive display engines to collaborators that execute tasks and create outcomes.
- Product promise: chat with pages, act in the browser session, create web apps, and use Cards to package recurring prompts.

## Customer Model

- Subscription browser for early adopters and AI power users.
- Opera opened public access after a Founders phase, implying community-shaped product development rather than enterprise procurement first.
- The premium model differentiates from free Edge/Chrome AI but raises the bar for reliability.

## Who They Capture

- Users who want a refined consumer browser brand around agentic workflows.
- Prompt-heavy users who see value in Cards and mode-specific AI workspaces.
- Privacy-aware users who prefer task execution in their local browser session.

## Who They Miss

- Mainstream users who do not want to pay for an experimental browser.
- Developers who prefer open-source, MCP, Playwright, or direct automation primitives.
- Users who want one simple assistant rather than several AI modes.
