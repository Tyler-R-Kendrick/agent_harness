# Opera Neon Gossip

## Positive Signals

- Opera has years of browser-product credibility and is using Neon as a flagship AI browser rather than a small feature.
- Official posts emphasize local in-browser execution for Do, Cards for repeatable prompting, and cloud agents for Make.
- Public access opened after a closed Founders period, which suggests Opera intentionally gathered early feedback before removing the waitlist.

## Negative Signals

- The Verge's trial run characterized the product as powerful but confusing, especially because Chat, Do, and Make have different capabilities and failure modes.
- Early hands-on commentary reports slow performance, wrong or missed page understanding, and situations where manual browsing remains faster.
- The subscription price makes "work in progress" roughness harder to accept.

## Bug And UX Risk Themes

- Mode confusion is a product risk: users need to know where to ask and what each agent can safely do.
- Cards can become a second abstraction users must learn before they see value.
- The browser must explain when work is local, when work is cloud-hosted, and what data each mode can access.
