# Agent360 Browser MCP Marketing

## Audience

- Claude Code and MCP power users who want AI control over their real Chrome profile.
- Developers automating authenticated SaaS tasks that need cookies, 2FA, CAPTCHA handling, or provider tokens.
- Builders frustrated by Playwright MCP's fresh browser sessions and BrowserMCP-style single-session limitations.

## Positioning

- "Your AI can't use a browser. Until now." frames the product as a missing primitive for coding agents.
- The marketing is explicitly comparative: real Chrome, logged-in sessions, CAPTCHA solving, multi-session isolation, and human-in-the-loop controls.
- It sells practical production blockers rather than generic "browser automation."

## Customer Model

- Visible model is free/open-source adoption through Chrome Web Store and `npx` installation.
- Likely monetization paths would be enterprise policy, support, audited builds, managed updates, or paid provider integrations.

## Who They Capture

- Individual developers and small teams using Claude Code for real SaaS workflows.
- Users who need a browser automation layer that can pause for human judgment.
- Teams evaluating MCP browser control before committing to managed cloud browsers.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprises uncomfortable with broad extension permissions, debugger access, cookie access, or token extraction.
- CI-first teams that need disposable browsers, repeatable runs, and formal replay artifacts.
- Non-Chrome and non-MCP users.
