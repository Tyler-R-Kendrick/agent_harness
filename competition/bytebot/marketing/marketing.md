# Bytebot Marketing

## Audience

- AI engineers who want a self-hosted desktop agent rather than a cloud browser farm.
- Operations teams with workflows spanning websites, documents, downloads, terminals, and office apps.
- Platform teams that prefer Docker, Railway, Helm, or cloud deployment over vendor-hosted sessions.

## Positioning

Bytebot sells "desktop agents" as the missing link between LLMs and real work. The main contrast is with browser-only agents and traditional RPA: Bytebot claims a complete computer can operate any software like a person.

## Customer Model

- Open-source project and GitHub-led adoption.
- Self-hosted deployment paths for local Docker, Railway, Kubernetes, and cloud providers.
- Likely monetization wedge is hosted/managed infrastructure, enterprise deployment support, or cloud scale for desktop-agent workloads.

## Capture

- Captures teams that need one agent surface for browser work, files, PDFs, spreadsheets, code editors, and terminals.
- Captures security-sensitive teams that want local or private-cloud control.
- Captures buyers who already understand remote desktops and VNC-style intervention.

## Miss

- Misses users who need a lightweight daily browser copilot.
- Misses teams that want managed browser infrastructure without owning container operations.
- Misses workflows where tab-scoped permissions, browser replay, and UI-specific evidence are more important than whole-computer compatibility.
