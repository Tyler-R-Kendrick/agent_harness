# OpenOwl Marketing

## Audience

- Developers, founders, growth teams, sales ops, analysts, researchers, and QA users with repetitive desktop or browser workflows.
- Local-first users who do not want screen contents routed through a proprietary cloud.
- MCP users who already run Claude Code, Codex, Gemini, or compatible assistants.

## Positioning

- Core claim: your AI assistant controls your desktop.
- Market frame: many useful workflows cross browsers, desktop apps, spreadsheets, email, and legacy dashboards where APIs are unavailable.
- Product promise: one-command install, open-source local control, no scripts, and plain-language automation.

## Customer Model

- Free self-hosted open-source usage.
- Low-price hosted/pro plan for unlimited tool calls and support.
- Growth likely comes through MCP communities, Product Hunt, Reddit demos, and developer word of mouth.

## Who They Capture

- Users who need browser plus desktop work rather than browser-only automation.
- Privacy-sensitive users attracted to local processing and open source.
- Operators who think in natural-language tasks rather than SDKs.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprise buyers needing mature governance, audit, policy, and procurement packaging.
- Teams requiring high-scale remote browser fleets.
- Developers who want deterministic Playwright-style code and checked-in tests.
