# Fellou Marketing

## Audience

- Productivity power users, analysts, marketers, students, job seekers, operators, and creators doing repetitive cross-app work.
- Early adopters who want a "Jarvis for everyone" experience rather than a passive browser plus chatbot.
- Users willing to trade setup/trust complexity for autonomous task execution.

## Positioning

- Core claim: "The World's First Agentic Browser."
- Market frame: chatbots answer, while Fellou acts across the web, desktop files, and personal workflows.
- Product promise: deep search, automated complex tasks, visible plans, real-time control, memory, and asynchronous work.

## Customer Model

- Official pages emphasize early access/download and broad productivity use cases.
- Third-party reviews report a credit/Sparks model with free trial capacity and paid tiers.
- Sponsored marketing claims more than 1,000,000 users and an 80% Online-Mind2web benchmark result, but those claims need independent validation before being treated as product proof.

## Who They Capture

- Users who feel trapped as "human middleware" between browser tabs, notes, email, calendars, and SaaS tools.
- Researchers and marketers who value generated reports more than raw search-result lists.
- Buyers attracted to visible autonomy controls rather than pure chat.

## Who They Miss

- Users who want a stable daily browser with minimal cognitive overhead.
- Enterprises that need formal governance, audit logs, and security review before granting browser/file/app access.
- Privacy-sensitive users who are uncomfortable giving an AI agent logged-in web and desktop context.
