# Tandem Browser Marketing

## Audience

- AI power users who already run Claude, GPT, Gemini, OpenClaw, Ollama, or mixed local/remote agent stacks.
- MCP builders who want a serious browser surface instead of custom wrappers or a headless automation library.
- Privacy-minded operators who want browser-agent work to stay local and inspectable.
- Teams experimenting with secure browser workflows, real authenticated sessions, and human-in-the-loop recovery.

## Positioning

- Core claim: the browser becomes a shared human-AI workspace, not a chatbot sidebar or detached automation target.
- Competitive frame: Comet and Dia are polished model-vendor or consumer AI browsers; Browser Use and Playwright are automation libraries; WebMCP helps participating websites, while Tandem works across the existing web.
- Trust frame: open source, MIT license, no backend, no telemetry, private connectivity, and named security layers.

## Customer Model

- Free open-source desktop browser.
- GitHub sponsorship funds development with supporter, power user, and champion tiers.
- No visible enterprise SKU at snapshot time, but the site explicitly mentions companies exploring secure browser-agent workflows.

## Who They Capture

- Developers and power users who distrust cloud browser agents with logged-in work.
- Users who want to co-drive rather than fully delegate browser tasks.
- Agent builders who want a local browser to be the shared execution substrate behind any model.

## Who They Miss

- Mainstream users who want polished consumer onboarding, brand trust, and minimal technical vocabulary.
- Enterprises needing procurement, admin console, SSO, audit retention, and formal support.
- Teams that prefer hosted browser infrastructure and do not want to manage desktop installs.
