# Automa Marketing

## Audience

- Technical no-code users, makers, scrapers, QA helpers, and productivity users who want free local browser automation.
- Developers or power users willing to reason about blocks, selectors, variables, loops, and JavaScript when needed.
- Users who prefer open-source extension tooling over managed SaaS automation.

## Positioning

- Core claim: low-code/no-code browser automation for typing, clicking, retrieving data, screenshots, scraping, and scheduled tasks.
- Market frame: browser actions can be modeled as reusable blocks connected into workflows.
- Product promise: automate repetitive web work locally through an extension, with enough block primitives to cover simple and complex flows.

## Customer Model

- Free Chrome Web Store distribution plus GitHub open-source adoption.
- Community support and issue reporting are central to the product loop.
- The model captures adoption more than enterprise monetization; conversion pressure is low compared with credit-based SaaS tools.

## Who They Capture

- Users who need a free, local, general-purpose automation builder.
- Power users who value visual blocks but still want JavaScript, variables, tables, and workflow sharing.
- Teams evaluating whether browser automation is useful before buying hosted agent infrastructure.

## Who They Miss

- Enterprise buyers needing a support SLA, role-based approvals, audit trails, managed credentials, or centralized run evidence.
- Agent developers who need first-class MCP/SDK endpoints and deterministic machine-readable observations.
- Beginners who want AI to infer the whole workflow rather than learning block-level automation.
