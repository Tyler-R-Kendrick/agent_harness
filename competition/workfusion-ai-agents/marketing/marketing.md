# WorkFusion AI Agents Marketing

## Audience

- Banks, financial institutions, and financial-crime compliance operations teams.
- Compliance executives trying to reduce false positives, analyst workload, and operational cost.
- Regulated buyers that need risk mitigation and explainability more than generic automation flexibility.

## Positioning

- WorkFusion positions AI agents as collaborative people-work automation for FCC, not as bots or generic GenAI.
- It leans into a regulated-domain proof point: sanctions, adverse media, AML, KYC, and transaction monitoring.
- The company argues that FCC is a showcase for AI agents because outcomes are measurable and already in production.

## Customer Model

- Vertical enterprise sales into financial services.
- Prebuilt named digital workers and solution briefs rather than generic developer APIs.
- Value proof through operational metrics, analyst-capacity claims, and bank case studies.

## Captures

- Buyers with known FCC pain and large alert volumes.
- Teams that want packaged domain expertise rather than building agent workflows themselves.
- Regulated organizations that need explainability and governed human review.

## Misses

- Developer teams looking for browser automation infrastructure.
- Horizontal operations teams outside WorkFusion's strongest FCC wedge.
- Users who want a local interactive browser-agent surface with screenshots, session replay, and prompt-level tuning.
