# WorkFusion AI Agents Design

## Look And Feel

- Vertical enterprise RegTech design: named agents, compliance workflows, analyst augmentation, and measurable operations outcomes.
- Marketing pages use financial-crime language rather than generic browser automation language.
- Product artifacts are closer to solution briefs and case studies than interactive developer quickstarts.

## Design Tokens To Track

```yaml
surface: industry solution pages, reports, case studies, named AI agents
accent: compliance/financial-services enterprise palette
primary_control: prebuilt agent deployment into FCC operations
core_objects:
  - sanctions alert
  - adverse media alert
  - KYC review
  - transaction monitoring investigation
  - AI agent
  - human analyst
  - explainability trail
information_density: medium
trust_model: domain specificity, measurable compliance outcomes, risk mitigation
```

## Differentiators

- WorkFusion sells named digital workers for a specific domain rather than generic browser or RPA primitives.
- The company claims production scale in sanctions and adverse media alert processing.
- It explicitly distinguishes FCC AI agents from generic bots and from unconstrained GenAI.

## What Is Good

- Clear buyer and workflow focus reduces ambiguity.
- Domain packaging helps justify value with metrics such as manual-work reduction, alert auto-adjudication, and false-positive reduction.
- Compliance and explainability are part of the value proposition, not afterthoughts.

## Where It Breaks Down

- Narrow vertical focus makes the product less relevant for broad browser-agent developer workflows.
- The design is less self-serve and less inspectable than developer platforms with live browser sessions and traces.
- Buyers outside banking/FCC may not see a path from the product to their own workflows.
