# Airtop Marketing

## Audience

- Operators and automation builders who want web tasks automated without owning browser infrastructure.
- Developers integrating agents into SaaS products that need shared auth, live view, scraping, proxies, and browser control.
- Business teams in sales, HR, finance, marketing, operations, research, and customer service.

## Positioning

- Core claim: give AI agents access to the web through cloud browsers.
- Market frame: many important business tasks require real web UI interaction because APIs are missing, incomplete, or too constrained.
- Product promise: describe the desired work, connect authentication, and run browser automations at scale.

## Customer Model

- Free plan for initial adoption.
- Paid tiers gate simultaneous sessions, published agents, proxy options, support, and compliance.
- Enterprise plan adds larger concurrency, SOC 2 Type 2 report access, and dedicated automation support.

## Who They Capture

- Teams that want useful automations quickly and value templates over framework control.
- SaaS builders that need embedded live view and shared auth for user-delegated tasks.
- Non-expert business users who need visible workflow examples.

## Who They Miss

- Local-first or self-hosting buyers.
- Developers who need full trace ownership, reproducible test fixtures, and checked-in deterministic scripts.
- Users who reject credit-based AI operation accounting or want to avoid cloud handling of authenticated sessions.
