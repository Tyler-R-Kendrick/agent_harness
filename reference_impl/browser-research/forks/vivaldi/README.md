# Vivaldi

## Overview
Explicitly refusing AI integration despite industry trend. Marketing positioning: "Keep Browsing Human." Investing strategic resources into Tab Tiling and advanced tab management instead. Counter-positioning strategy works as profitable niche for power users skeptical of AI.

## Technical Approach
- **Base**: Chromium
- **AI Integration**: None (intentional)
- **Key Differentiator**: Anti-AI positioning; advanced tab management and tiling UI

## Key Learnings
- Counter-positioning against industry trend (no AI) can work as profitable niche
- Tab management remains valuable feature space despite AI focus elsewhere
- "Keep Browsing Human" resonates with vocal segment of power users
- Research shows AI summaries reduce source clicks—data supports concern

## UX Innovations
- Advanced tab tiling and spatial layout
- Manual tab organization tools vs AI-driven organization
- Power user-focused feature set
- Privacy-preserving design (no AI telemetry)

## Risks & Concerns
- Niche market limits addressable scale
- "Keep Browsing Human" messaging could become dated if AI becomes ubiquitous
- Competing against much larger players with differentiation strategy alone
- Research showing AI reduces clicks could create headwind if ignored

## Notes
Vivaldi's explicit refusal of AI is bold counter-positioning. Research showing AI summaries reduce source clicks provides data-driven rationale. Sustainable as niche player for power users, but unlikely to capture mainstream market. Tab Tiling focus is defensible but narrow.
