# Ungoogled-Chromium

## Overview
Privacy-focused Chromium fork with all Google services and telemetry removed. Very active community maintenance. Patch-based approach to modifications makes it good reference for maintaining forks over time. Strong use among privacy advocates and Linux communities.

## Technical Approach
- **Base**: Chromium fork
- **AI Integration**: None (privacy-focused)
- **Key Differentiator**: Google service removal; telemetry elimination; patch-based maintenance model

## Key Learnings
- Patch-based approach is scalable for fork maintenance
- Privacy differentiation has sustainable niche market
- Community maintenance model can work for forks
- Clean separation of concerns (patches vs binary) aids maintenance

## UX Innovations
- No Google telemetry
- No Google services integration
- Cleaner data footprint
- Privacy-preserving defaults

## Risks & Concerns
- Chromium update cadence requires continuous rebasing
- Patch conflicts with upstream increase over time
- Community maintenance is volunteer effort (sustainability risk)
- Feature parity with stock Chromium minus Google services
